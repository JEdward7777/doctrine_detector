Baptism, according to New Testament teachings, symbolizes the cleansing of sin and the initiation into the community of believers. It is seen as an outward expression of one's inward faith in Jesus Christ and a public declaration of one's commitment to follow him. Additionally, baptism is also seen as a way to demonstrate obedience to Jesus' command to be baptized and to publicly identify with his death, burial, and resurrection.

It is important to note that different Christian denominations may have varying interpretations and practices surrounding baptism, but generally speaking, it is considered a fundamental sacrament in the Christian faith.
