The term "Zion" is used in a few different ways in the Bible, both literally and figuratively. 

1. Literally, Zion originally referred to the hill in Jerusalem on which the city of David was built. In the Old Testament, Zion is often used as a synonym for Jerusalem or the city of God. It was seen as a symbol of God's presence and blessing among his people.

2. Figuratively, Zion is used to represent the people of God, specifically the faithful community who worship and serve Him. It can symbolize God's kingdom, salvation, and deliverance.

3. In the New Testament, Zion is used metaphorically to refer to the heavenly Jerusalem, the city of God that believers look forward to in the future. It represents the ultimate fulfillment of God's promises and the eternal dwelling place of His people.

Overall, the term "Zion" is rich in symbolism and carries the idea of God's presence, blessing, salvation, and ultimate victory for His people.
