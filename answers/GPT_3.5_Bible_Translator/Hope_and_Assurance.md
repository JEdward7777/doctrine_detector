When a person has no hope, it can indicate a state of despair, darkness, and a sense of hopelessness.

Without hope, individuals may struggle to find purpose, meaning, and direction in life. In the Bible, the absence of hope is often associated with feelings of emptiness, fear, and uncertainty. For example, in the book of Ephesians 2:12, the apostle Paul describes the Gentiles as being "without hope and without God in the world" before coming to faith in Christ.
