The Temple was seen as a more fitting and permanent location for worshipping God compared to the portable tabernacle that had been used during the Israelites' time in the wilderness. Additionally, the tabernacle was considered a precursor to the Temple, and once the Temple was constructed, it became the focal point for worship and religious ceremonies for the Israelites.
