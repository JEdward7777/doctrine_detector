In Islam, we believe in the oneness of God (Tawheed) as a fundamental principle of faith. The concept of God the Father, God the Son, and God the Holy Spirit is a Christian belief known as the Trinity. This concept asserts that there is one God who exists in three persons – the Father, the Son (Jesus Christ), and the Holy Spirit – each fully God, yet distinct from each other.

From an Islamic perspective, we do not believe in the Trinity, as it contradicts the concept of Tawheed. In Islam, Allah (God) is One and indivisible with no partners or associates. The Quran explicitly denies the idea of God having any partners or being divided into multiple entities. Allah says in the Quran, "Say: He is Allah, [who is] One, Allah, the Eternal Refuge. He neither begets nor is born, nor is there to Him any equivalent" (Surah Al-Ikhlas, 112:1-4).

Therefore, for Muslims, God is not divided into different persons or entities with distinct essences. Allah is uniquely and singularly God, with no partners, sons, or associates.
