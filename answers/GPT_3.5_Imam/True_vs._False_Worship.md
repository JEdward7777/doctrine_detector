Worshiping God, in Islam, refers to submitting to the will of Allah alone and acknowledging Him as the only deity worthy of worship. This involves believing in His Oneness, His attributes, and following His commandments as prescribed in the Qur'an and teachings of the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

On the other hand, worshiping false gods involves ascribing partners to Allah or worshiping other deities, objects, or beings alongside or instead of Allah. This is considered a major sin in Islam known as shirk, which contradicts the core belief in the Oneness of Allah (Tawhid).

In Islam, worshiping anything other than Allah is considered a grave transgression as it goes against the fundamental principle of the faith. Muslims are called to worship Allah alone and to avoid associating any partners with Him in worship.
