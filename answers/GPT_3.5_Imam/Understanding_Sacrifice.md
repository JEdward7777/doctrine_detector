In Islam, Jesus (peace be upon him) is considered one of the great prophets of Allah. The concept of him being referred to as the 'Lamb of God' in relation to sacrifices commanded in the Old Testament is a Christian belief. In Christianity, this term symbolizes Jesus as the ultimate sacrificial Lamb who was offered as a final atonement for the sins of humanity.

Muslims believe in the concept of sacrifice as well, but our perspective is different. In Islam, the act of sacrificing animals during Eid al-Adha (Festival of Sacrifice) is a way to commemorate the willingness of Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) to sacrifice his son Isma'il (Ishmael) in obedience to Allah's command. This event signifies submission to Allah's will and highlights the importance of faith and devotion.

While Muslims acknowledge the significance of Jesus in our faith and revere him as a prophet, we do not hold the belief that he was the ultimate sacrificial offering for the atonement of sins. Instead, we believe in seeking forgiveness directly from Allah through sincere repentance, good deeds, and following the teachings of the Prophets, including Jesus.
