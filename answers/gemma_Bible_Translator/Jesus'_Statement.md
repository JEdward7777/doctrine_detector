## Question [Jesus' Statement](../../questions/Jesus'_Statement.md)
What did Jesus mean when he said, 'I am the Resurrection and the Life'?

## Target answer from notes
When Jesus said, 'I am the Resurrection and the Life,' he meant that he is the source of resurrection and the one who causes people to come back to life.

## Concern to grade by
Clarifying Jesus' identity and role in resurrection is important for faith and theological understanding.

## Answer given by [gemma Bible Translator](../../answering_models/gemma_Bible_Translator.md)
**Jesus' statement, "I am the Resurrection and the Life," reveals two key aspects of his divine nature:**

**1. The Resurrection:**

* This refers to Jesus' physical resurrection from the dead after his crucifixion.
* His resurrection demonstrates God's power over death and offers eternal life to all who believe in him.
* The resurrection validates Jesus' claim to be the Son of God and the promised Messiah.

**2. The Life:**

* This speaks to Jesus being the source of eternal life for all who believe in him.
* He offers spiritual life to the soul, which transcends physical death.
* Jesus is the fulfillment of God's promise of life abundant (John 10:10).

**Biblical references supporting this statement:**

* **John 11:25:** "I am the resurrection and the life. He who believes in me, though he die, yet shall he live."
* **Revelation 20:6:** "Blessed and holy is he who has part in the first resurrection: on such the second death has no power."

**Theological interpretation:**

* Jesus' resurrection and life-giving power are central to Christianity.
* His sacrifice and resurrection offer forgiveness of sins and eternal life to all who believe in him.
* He is the ultimate expression of God's love and grace.

**In summary, Jesus' statement "I am the Resurrection and the Life" means that he is the source of both physical resurrection and spiritual life for all who believe in him.**
