What does the term 'majesty' signify in relation to God's character?

## Answer given by [gemma Bible Translator](../../answering_models/gemma_Bible_Translator.md)
**The term "majesty" in relation to God's character signifies:**

**1. Divine Glory and Honor:**

* Majesty refers to the profound glory and honor that belong exclusively to God.
* It encompasses God's supreme power, wisdom, righteousness, and love.

**2. Transcendent Glory:**

* God's majesty transcends human comprehension and imagination.
* His glory is infinite, eternal, and utterly surpassing anything created by human hands.

**3. Royal Character:**

* God is often depicted as a king, and his majesty reflects his royal authority and sovereignty.
* His sovereignty extends over all creation, and his kingly power is characterized by justice, mercy, and love.

**4. Divine Majesty as Majesty of Grace:**

* God's majesty is not merely a display of power or authority.
* It is primarily a manifestation of God's boundless love and grace.
* His majestic grace is revealed in his willingness to redeem humanity through Jesus Christ.

**5. A Call to Worship and Awe:**

* The revelation of God's majesty should inspire awe and reverence in the hearts of believers.
* It reminds us of God's greatness and sovereignty, and encourages us to approach him with humility and faith.
