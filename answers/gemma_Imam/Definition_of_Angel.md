**Biblical Definition of an Angel:**

The biblical definition of an angel is a spiritual being created by Allah (God) who carries out His commands and interacts with the physical realm. Angels are described as:

* Messengers and messengers of Allah (Quran 2:97)
* Spirits that can ascend and descend (Quran 7:53)
* Servants of Allah who do His bidding (Quran 6:102)

**Primary Role:**

Angels have various primary roles in the Bible, including:

**1. Messengers:**
* Angels deliver prophecies, revelations, and instructions from Allah to prophets and messengers.
* They convey Allah's words and commands to His servants.

**2. Guardians:**
* Angels are assigned to individuals, families, and nations to protect and guide them.
* They stand beside believers and offer support and encouragement.

**3. Intermediaries:**
* Angels can mediate between Allah and humanity.
* They carry prayers, supplications, and sacrifices to Allah.

**4. Witnesses:**
* Angels are often mentioned as witnesses to significant events in biblical history.
* Their presence signifies Allah's involvement and approval.

**5. Spiritual Beings:**
* Angels exist in a spiritual realm but can interact with the physical realm.
* They can appear in physical form and perform miraculous deeds.

**6. Companions:**
* Angels are described as companions of the righteous in the afterlife.
* They are said to share in the joy and peace of paradise.
