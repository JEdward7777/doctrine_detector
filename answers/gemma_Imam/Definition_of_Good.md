**Biblical Definition of 'Good':**

The Bible defines 'good' as that which is:

* Beneficial to oneself or others
* Ethical and moral
* Pleasing to God
* Characterized by positive attributes such as love, mercy, kindness, and forgiveness

**Islamic Concept of 'Evil':**

In Islam, 'evil' is defined as:

* Anything that contradicts Islamic principles of faith and morality
* Actions that harm individuals or society
* Negative attributes like malice, cruelty, injustice, and corruption

**Contrasting the Concepts:**

**1. Focus:**

* The Biblical definition emphasizes the pursuit of goodness for both oneself and others.
* The Islamic definition focuses on the avoidance of evil and the promotion of righteousness.

**2. Religious Perspective:**

* The Bible defines 'good' from a Christian perspective, while the Islamic definition is based on Islamic beliefs and values.

**3. Attribute:**

* The Biblical definition of 'good' is often associated with attributes like love and mercy.
* The Islamic definition emphasizes the avoidance of negative attributes like malice and cruelty.

**4. Consequences:**

* The Bible teaches that good deeds will be rewarded in heaven, while evil deeds will be punished.
* Islam emphasizes the accountability for actions in both this life and the afterlife.

**5. Practical Implications:**

* The Biblical definition encourages acts of kindness and charity.
* The Islamic definition promotes ethical behavior and social justice.

**Similarities:**

* Both concepts recognize the existence of moral distinctions.
* Both definitions emphasize the importance of personal responsibility for actions.
* Both concepts ultimately seek to promote a just and peaceful society.

**Conclusion:**

While there are some differences in the Biblical and Islamic definitions of 'good' and 'evil,' both concepts share similarities in their emphasis on the pursuit of righteousness and the avoidance of harm.
