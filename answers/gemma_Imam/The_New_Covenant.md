What does it mean that in the New Covenant, God would write His law on the people's hearts?

## Target answer from notes
It signifies a transformative relationship with God where His laws are internalized, fostering a personal knowledge of Him and an intrinsic motivation to follow His will.

## Concern to grade by
Grasping the transition from the Old Testament law to the principles of the New Covenant.

## Answer given by [gemma Imam](../../answering_models/gemma_Imam.md)
**In the New Covenant, God would write His law on the people's hearts** is a symbolic representation of the transformative power of faith in Jesus Christ. It does not suggest a literal rewriting of physical hearts.

**Biblical Interpretation:**

* This passage is referring to the spiritual transformation that occurs in believers.
* The law written on the heart represents the ethical and moral principles that God desires to instill in the hearts of His followers.
* The New Covenant emphasizes an intimate and personal relationship with God based on love and grace, rather than strict legalism.

**Islamic Perspective:**

* The concept of hearts being illuminated with divine knowledge aligns with the Islamic belief in the role of the Holy Spirit (Ruh al-Qudus) in guiding and empowering believers.
* In Islam, the Quran and Sunnah serve as external sources of guidance, while the inner transformation of the heart is crucial for righteous actions.

**Meaning in the Context of Islam:**

* The New Covenant concept reflects the Islamic belief in the universality of faith and the potential for transformation among all people.
* It highlights the importance of spiritual growth and the development of moral character in the Muslim community.

**Conclusion:**

The passage from the New Covenant about God writing His law on the people's hearts is a symbolic representation of the transformative power of faith in Jesus Christ, emphasizing the importance of spiritual growth and ethical transformation among believers.
