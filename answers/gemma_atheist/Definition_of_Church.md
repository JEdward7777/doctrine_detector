What does the term 'church' refer to in the New Testament?

## Answer given by [gemma atheist](../../answering_models/gemma_atheist.md)
**In the New Testament, the term "church" primarily refers to the community of believers assembled and empowered by the Holy Spirit.** This understanding transcends any specific physical structure or hierarchical organization.

**Biblical References:**

* **Early Church:** The apostles and early believers referred to their gathering as the "church" (Acts 2:42).
* **Universal Assembly:** Paul used the term "church" to describe the universal assembly of believers from every nation (Ephesians 1:22-23).
* **Local Community:** In several passages, "church" refers to local communities of believers (1 Corinthians 1:2; Colossians 4:15).

**Cultural Context:**

* The term "church" in the New Testament is primarily used in a cultural context where religious communities were often associated with physical structures like temples or meeting houses.
* The early Christians emphasized the spiritual nature of the church, emphasizing the unity and fellowship of believers rather than any physical building.

**Modern Interpretation:**

* Today, the term "church" can refer to both physical buildings and the communities that gather in them.
* It is important to note that the New Testament concept of the church transcends cultural and denominational boundaries.

**Conclusion:**

In the New Testament, "church" primarily refers to the community of believers assembled and empowered by the Holy Spirit, emphasizing the spiritual nature of the early Christian movement rather than any physical structure.
