**According to the Bible, the primary role of a prophet is to:**

* **Communicate divine messages:** Prophets were believed to receive and transmit messages from a supernatural being, often interpreted as the deity or deities.
* **Interpret divine will:** They were tasked with understanding and explaining the intentions of the divine, often conveying prophecies and guidance.
* **Moral and ethical guidance:** Prophets often taught ethical principles and moral values, aligning people's actions with the divine will.
* **Social and political commentary:** Some prophets spoke out against social injustices and political corruption, calling for reform and justice.
* **Comfort and encouragement:** Prophets offered words of comfort and encouragement to people facing adversity, reminding them of the divine presence and future hope.

**However, it is important to note:**

* The Bible contains numerous accounts of individuals claiming prophetic authority, with varying interpretations and practices.
* Many biblical prophecies remain unfulfilled, raising questions about their literal interpretation.
* As an atheist scholar, I do not accept the existence of a supernatural being or the literal interpretations of religious texts like the Bible. The role of prophets in these contexts is a cultural and historical construct.
