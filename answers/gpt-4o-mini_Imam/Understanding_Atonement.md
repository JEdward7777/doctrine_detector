1. **Sacrificial System**: In the Old Testament, the concept of atonement was frequently demonstrated through the sacrificial system, where animals were offered to God to atone for the sins of the people. This foreshadowed Jesus’ ultimate sacrifice. Christians believe Jesus is the "Lamb of God," who fulfills and transcends the need for such sacrifices by providing a perfect, once-for-all atonement.

2. **Relationship with God**: Through His death, Christians believe that Jesus paid the penalty for sin, allowing believers to be forgiven and reconciled with God. This act restores the broken relationship between humanity and the Creator.

3. **Grace and Faith**: Christian doctrine emphasizes that salvation and forgiveness from sin cannot be earned through human efforts but is a gift from God that is received through faith in Jesus Christ. His death is seen as a demonstration of God's love and grace, highlighting the core Christian belief that salvation is available to all who believe in Him.

4. **Resurrection**: The significance of Christ’s death is also closely tied to His resurrection. Christians believe that His rising from the dead confirms His victory over sin and death, providing hope for eternal life for all who have faith in Him.
