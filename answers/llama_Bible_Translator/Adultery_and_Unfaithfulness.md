In what way is the term 'unfaithful' related to the concept of marriage as described in the text?

## Answer given by [llama Bible Translator](../../answering_models/llama_Bible_Translator.md)
A great question!

The term "unfaithful" in the context of marriage refers to a spouse who betrays their commitment to their partner by engaging in acts of adultery, infidelity, or unchastity. This can include physical intimacy with someone other than their spouse, but it also extends to emotional and spiritual infidelity.

In the biblical narrative, God's design for marriage is one of mutual love, trust, and covenantal commitment between a man and woman (Genesis 2:24; Matthew 19:4-6). When a person enters into a marriage relationship with their spouse, they are making a sacred vow to be faithful to each other, just as God is faithful to His people.

In this sense, an "unfaithful" partner is one who breaks that covenantal commitment by engaging in behavior that contradicts the love and trust they have pledged to uphold. This can lead to deep hurt, betrayal, and damage to the marriage relationship (Matthew 5:27-32; Hebrews 13:4).

However, as a Christian Bible translator, I must emphasize that God's grace and mercy are available to restore broken relationships and heal the wounds of infidelity. Through repentance, forgiveness, and restoration, it is possible for an "unfaithful" partner to seek redemption and rebuild their relationship with their spouse (1 Corinthians 6:9-11; 2 Corinthians 7:10-11).
