 The term "begat" (or its Hebrew and Greek equivalents) is used in the Bible, particularly in the genealogical accounts, to denote a direct line of descent from one generation to another. It indicates that a specific individual fathered or begot a named offspring. This usage helps establish clear lines of family history and shows how certain individuals are connected to each other within the larger narrative of the Bible. However, it's essential to note that this term should not be taken literally in every case, as some genealogies may compress multiple generations into just a few names (for example, through use of "and" instead of "begat") or include individuals who were not biological ancestors but took on mentorship roles. Ultimately, the Bible's genealogies serve to demonstrate God's providence in guiding and preserving His chosen people throughout history.
