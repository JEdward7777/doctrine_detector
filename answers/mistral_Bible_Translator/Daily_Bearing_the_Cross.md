It symbolizes following Jesus closely, imitating His sacrifice, and accepting the difficulties that come with discipleship. This doesn't necessarily mean physical suffering or martyrdom in every instance but rather a willingness to endure personal hardships, challenges, and even rejection for one's faith in Christ. It is an invitation to carry the burden of obedience to God's will and the teachings of Jesus.
