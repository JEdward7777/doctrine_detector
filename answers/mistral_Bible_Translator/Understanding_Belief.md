What is the difference between believing in something and believing someone?

## Target answer from notes
To believe something is to accept or trust that it is true, while to believe in someone means to trust that person completely, acknowledging their identity and faithfulness.

## Answer given by [mistral Bible Translator](../../answering_models/mistral_Bible_Translator.md)
 Believing in something refers to accepting or having faith in an idea, concept, principle, or belief system without necessarily knowing if it's true. It can be based on personal experiences, cultural background, or intuition. This type of belief doesn't require a specific person or source.

On the other hand, believing someone involves trusting in the words, actions, and character of another individual. When we believe someone, we accept what they say as true because we believe them to be reliable, truthful, and honest. In the Christian context, this can extend to believing in God or Jesus based on their teachings and actions as presented in the Bible.
