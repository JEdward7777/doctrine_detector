 In the context of ancient Israelite worship, the altar held great significance as it was one of the key elements where religious rituals were performed. It served as a symbolic connection between God and His people. The altar was used for burnt offerings, peace offerings, sin offerings, and various other ceremonies outlined in the Hebrew Bible (Tanakh).

The altar was typically built on high ground, such as Mount Moriah where Solomon's Temple stood (2 Chronicles 3:1), or at the Tabernacle in the wilderness. The purpose of these offerings was to seek forgiveness for sins, express gratitude, and maintain a close relationship with God.
