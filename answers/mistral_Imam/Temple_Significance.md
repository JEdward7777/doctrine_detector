## Answer given by [mistral Imam](../../answering_models/mistral_Imam.md)
 In Islamic tradition, it is believed that the Temple referred to as the Bayt al-Maqdis (House of Peace) was built by King David, not Solomon. This temple is identified with the First Temple in Jewish history, which was said to have been destroyed by Nebuchadnezzar II of Babylon in 586 BCE.

According to Islamic sources, this temple was a place of worship for the Jewish people and was significant because it housed the Ark of the Covenant containing the Tablets of Stone upon which the Ten Commandments were written. It is also considered one of the three holy mosques mentioned in the Quran along with Masjid al-Aqsa (Noble Sanctuary) and Al-Masjid al-Haram (the Grand Mosque) in Mecca.

Regarding its purpose, as a place of worship for the Jewish people, it was where they could turn to Allah (God) and seek His blessings and guidance. The temple is also mentioned in several verses of the Quran, such as Surat Al-Isra' (17:1), which speaks about the Night Journey (Isra' and Mi'raj) of Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

Lastly, it should be noted that Islamic teachings encourage respect for all places of worship and their significance to different religious communities. As an Imam, I emphasize this respect and understanding towards the historical and religious importance of Bayt al-Maqdis for our Jewish brothers and sisters.
