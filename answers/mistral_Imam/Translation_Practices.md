In many Bible translations, the term "LORD" is used instead of the original Hebrew name Yahweh (יהוה) to maintain reverence while transliterating a divine name. This practice stems from Jewish tradition that considers the direct pronunciation of Yahweh as too sacred for casual use.

The capitalized English term "LORD" is often used in modern Bibles as a convention to represent the tetragrammaton (YHWH) found in the Hebrew Bible. The term "LORD" is also a translation of Adonai, which was substituted for Yahweh in Jewish texts when pronouncing the divine name was considered inappropriate.

This tradition has carried over into Christian translations as well, to maintain a consistent convention and reflect the Jewish roots of the Bible. However, it's important to note that various religious communities have different practices regarding the use and pronunciation of sacred names.
