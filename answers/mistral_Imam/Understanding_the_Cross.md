 In Christian beliefs, the crucifixion of Jesus (Isa in Islam) holds great significance as it is seen as the supreme act of sacrifice and atonement for humanity's sins. According to the Bible, Jesus was sent by God to redeem mankind from sin and provide a way to eternal life through his death and resurrection. The crucifixion demonstrates God's love and mercy for humanity, as well as the consequences of human sinfulness.

From an Islamic perspective, we believe in Jesus (Isa) but not in the Christian doctrine of the Trinity or the Crucifixion. According to Islam, Jesus was not crucified but instead was raised to heaven without dying on the cross. This is one of the fundamental differences between Christianity and Islam regarding Jesus's life and significance.
