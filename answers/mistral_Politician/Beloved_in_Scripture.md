## Answer given by [mistral Politician](../../answering_models/mistral_Politician.md)
 In the New Testament, the term 'beloved' or 'dear children' (often translated from Greek words "agapete" and "tekna") is used with great affection and respect. It serves as a symbol of love, care, and closeness between God and his people, as well as among the Christian community.

For example:
- Matthew 12:49 - "And stretching out His hand toward His disciples, He said, 'Here are My mother and My brothers! For whoever does the will of My Father in heaven, he is My brother and sister and mother.'" (NASB)
- John 13:34 - "A new commandment I give to you, that you love one another: just as I have loved you, you also are to love one another." (ESV)
- Colossians 4:12 - "Epaphras, who is one of your number, a bondslave of Christ Jesus, greets you, and so do Mark, Aristarchus, Demas, and Luke, my fellow laborers." (NASB)
