1. Judgment: The Day of Yahweh is typically seen as a day of judgment when God will intervene in human history to punish the wicked and establish His righteousness.

2. Restoration: Following the period of judgment, there is often a promise of restoration and renewal for the righteous, as well as the establishment of God's kingdom on Earth.

3. Cosmic disturbances: Some references suggest that the Day of Yahweh will be preceded by or accompanied by signs in the heavens, such as darkness, stars falling from the sky, and other natural disasters (e.g., Joel 2:30-31; Isaiah 13:9-10).

4. Return of God's people: The Day of Yahweh is often associated with the return of God's chosen people to their homeland or a gathering of believers in preparation for the fulfillment of divine promises (e.g., Isaiah 2:12; Zechariah 14:5).

5. Universal reign of God: Many references depict the Day of Yahweh as the time when God will reign over all nations and people, bringing an end to injustice, oppression, and evil (e.g., Micah 4:1-3; Zechariah 14:9).
