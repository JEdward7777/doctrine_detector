In the New Testament, the concept of "priesthood" refers to the spiritual role and authority granted to certain individuals within the early Christian community.

In 1 Peter 2:5, it is stated that "you also, like living stones, are being built into a spiritual house to be a holy priesthood, offering spiritual sacrifices acceptable to God through Jesus Christ." This passage suggests that every believer in Jesus has the potential to serve as a spiritual leader or priest by offering their lives in service to God and others.

However, it's important to note that the role of a priest in the New Testament is not synonymous with the role of a priest in the Old Testament or in contemporary religious traditions. In the New Testament, there is no longer a need for a specific group of people to perform sacrificial rites on behalf of others because Jesus, as the ultimate sacrifice, has fulfilled that role (Hebrews 10:1-14).

In conclusion, while every believer in Jesus may have a spiritual priesthood within themselves, this does not mean they are equivalent to a priest in a formal, institutional sense. It's always essential to approach religious texts with sensitivity and respect for their historical and cultural contexts.
