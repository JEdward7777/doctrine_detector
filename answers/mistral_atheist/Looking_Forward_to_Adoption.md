Romans 8:23 in the New Testament of the Bible reads, "And not only so, but we also who have the firstfruits of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting eagerly for our adoption as sons, the redemption of our body."

This verse is part of a larger discussion in Romans 8 about the relationship between creation (all things) and God. In this context, "adoption as sons" refers to the spiritual transformation that occurs in believers when they accept Jesus Christ. This adoption signifies that believers are no longer outsiders or enemies of God but are now considered His children with all the privileges and protection that come with it.

The phrase "waiting eagerly" suggests a sense of anticipation and longing, reflecting the hope for the full realization of God's promise. The "redemption of our body" is often interpreted as the future resurrection of believers when their physical bodies will be transformed to be free from sin and death.

In terms of what this means for believers, it emphasizes the importance of hope in the face of adversity, patience in waiting for God's promises to be fulfilled, and faithfulness in living according to God's will while awaiting their ultimate redemption.
