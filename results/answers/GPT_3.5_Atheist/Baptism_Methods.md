In the Bible, baptism is typically understood as a ritual cleansing or being initiated into the Christian faith. The New Testament mentions a few methods of baptism:

1. **Immersion**: This is the most widely practiced form of baptism in many Christian traditions. It involves completely submerging the individual in water, symbolizing burial and resurrection with Christ.

2. **Affusion**: This method involves pouring water over the head of the individual, symbolizing purification and renewal.

3. **Aspersion**: This form of baptism involves sprinkling water on the individual, symbolizing the cleansing power of the Holy Spirit.

Interpretations of these different methods of baptism vary among Christian denominations. Some churches believe that immersion is necessary for a valid baptism, as it closely mirrors the baptism of Jesus by John the Baptist in the river Jordan. Other churches accept affusion or aspersion as valid forms of baptism, emphasizing the symbolism of purification and initiation into the faith over the method of water application.
