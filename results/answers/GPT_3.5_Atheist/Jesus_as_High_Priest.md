In Christianity, Jesus is often referred to as the "High Priest" or the "eternal High Priest." The concept of Jesus as the High Priest has significant implications for the sacrifices made by human priests. However, with the belief in Jesus as the ultimate High Priest who offered himself as a sacrifice, the need for animal sacrifices and human priests to intercede on behalf of the people is considered obsolete.

Therefore, in Christianity, the sacrificial system of the priesthood is seen as fulfilled and surpassed by Jesus' sacrificial death on the cross.
