In the Bible, a covenant is a solemn agreement, promise, or contract between God and His people. It is a binding relationship based on mutual commitments and obligations. In the Old Testament, God made several covenants with different individuals and communities, such as the Abrahamic Covenant, the Mosaic Covenant, and the Davidic Covenant. These covenants established God's promises, expectations, and blessings for His people.

In the New Testament, Jesus inaugurated a new covenant through His sacrificial death and resurrection, which offers salvation and forgiveness of sins to all who believe in Him. The new covenant is based on grace, faith, and the indwelling of the Holy Spirit, rather than on strict obedience to the Mosaic Law. It is a covenant of love and redemption that transcends cultural and ethnic boundaries.
