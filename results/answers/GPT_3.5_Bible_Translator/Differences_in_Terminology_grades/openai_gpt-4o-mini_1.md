1. Exhort:
The term "exhort" means to strongly urge or encourage someone to do something. In the New Testament, exhortation often involves a call to action, a challenge to maintain faithfulness, or a reminder of important truths. It is typically meant to inspire, motivate, and uplift believers in their walk with God. For example, the Apostle Paul frequently exhorted believers to stand firm in their faith and to live in a manner worthy of the gospel.

2. Encourage:
"Encourage" is a term that conveys comfort, solace, and support to someone who may be facing challenges or difficulties. In the Bible, encouragement is often given to lift up the spirits of believers, remind them of God's promises, and strengthen them in times of trouble. For instance, in 1 Thessalonians 5:11, Paul encourages believers to build each other up and to comfort one another.

3. Admonish:
"Admonish" carries a sense of caution, correction, or warning. It is often used to address wrongdoing, offer guidance, or point out areas where someone may need to change their behavior. Admonition in the context of Christian theology is meant to help believers grow in holiness, avoid sin, and stay on the path of righteousness. For example, in Colossians 3:16, Paul instructs believers to admonish one another with all wisdom.

In summary, while exhortation emphasizes motivation and challenge, encouragement focuses on support and comfort, and admonition highlights correction and warning. Each of these terms plays a valuable role in guiding and nurturing believers in their faith journey.
