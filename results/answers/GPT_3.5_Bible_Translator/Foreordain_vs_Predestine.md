In Christian teachings, the terms "foreordain" and "predestine" are related but have slightly different connotations. 

Foreordain generally refers to God's plan or purpose that has been established beforehand. It emphasizes God's sovereignty and his ability to ordain or plan events in advance.

Predestine, on the other hand, refers to God's predetermined plan for individuals regarding their salvation or destiny. It is the belief that God has chosen certain individuals to be saved and others to be condemned before the foundation of the world.

Both concepts involve the idea of God's divine plan and sovereignty over all things, but predestination specifically deals with individual salvation whereas foreordination can refer to a broader scope of events and outcomes.
