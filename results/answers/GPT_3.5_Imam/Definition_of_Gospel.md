The term 'gospel' comes from the Old English word 'godspell', which means 'good news' or 'glad tidings'. In the context of the Bible, the term 'gospel' refers to the accounts of the life, teachings, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ as recorded in the four Gospels of the New Testament: Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John.

The Gospels are considered the heart of the Christian message and provide a record of Jesus' ministry on earth, his miracles, his teachings, and the fulfillment of Old Testament prophecies regarding the Messiah. The Gospels also emphasize the significance of Jesus' death and resurrection as the means of salvation for humanity.

As Muslims, while we do not view the Gospels in the same way as Christians do, we do believe in the importance of Jesus (peace be upon him) as a prophet and messenger of God. Muslims also believe in the Injil (Gospel) as a revealed book given to Jesus, although the exact nature and content of the Injil are not known to us today.
