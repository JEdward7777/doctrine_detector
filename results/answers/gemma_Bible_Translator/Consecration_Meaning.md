**Biblical Examples:**

* **Bread and wine:** In the Eucharist, bread and wine are consecrated by the priest, becoming the body and blood of Christ. (1 Corinthians 11:24)
* **Temple vessels:** The utensils and furniture used in ancient temple worship were consecrated, symbolizing their sacredness. (Exodus 30:33)
* **Leaders:** Certain individuals were consecrated for specific roles in the church, such as bishops and deacons. (1 Timothy 3:1-7)

**Christian Theology:**

Consecration is seen as a transformative act that sets something or someone apart for a divine purpose. It involves:

* **Separation:** Distancing something or someone from the ordinary or profane.
* **Purification:** Cleaning or purifying something or someone for sacred use.
* **Blessing:** Infusing something or someone with divine significance and power.

**Purpose of Consecration:**

* To set something or someone apart for a specific use in God's service.
* To symbolize their spiritual significance or holiness.
* To empower them for their divine purpose.
