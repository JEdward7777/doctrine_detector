**Biblical Definition of a Covenant:**

A covenant in the Bible is a legal and relational agreement between two or more parties, established upon specific terms and conditions. It involves:

**1. Intentionality:** A conscious decision to enter into the agreement.
**2. Mutuality:** Involvement of two or more parties.
**3. Explicit terms:** Specific conditions and stipulations.
**4. Exchange:** A reciprocal action or promise.
**5. Witness:** Often involves the presence of witnesses to ensure accountability.

**Biblical Examples of Covenants:**

* **Covenant of Works:** God's original covenant with Adam, based on obedience and sacrifice.
* **Covenant of Grace:** God's new covenant with Abraham, centered on faith in Jesus Christ.
* **Covenant of Law:** The Ten Commandments and subsequent laws given to Moses.
* **Church Covenant:** The commitment of believers to live in community and support one another.

**Biblical Principles of Covenants:**

* Covenants are based on God's faithfulness and grace.
* Covenants are unconditional in God's eyes, but may have conditional consequences for humans.
* Covenants are eternal and cannot be broken by God.
* Covenants are meant to bring blessing and redemption.
