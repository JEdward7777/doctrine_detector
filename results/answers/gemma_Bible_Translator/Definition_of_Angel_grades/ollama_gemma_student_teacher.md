## Biblical Definition of an Angel:

The biblical definition of an angel is a complex and multifaceted one, spanning various contexts and literary genres. However, some key elements emerge:

* **Messengers:** Angels are primarily described as messengers, carrying divine pronouncements and instructions to individuals or groups (Genesis 22:15, Daniel 7:10).
* **Servants:** Angels serve God faithfully, assisting in tasks such as worship (Psalm 103:20), deliverance (Daniel 3:24), and warfare (Angels are often depicted as warriors in the Book of Revelation).
* **Created beings:** The Bible suggests that angels were created by God (Genesis 1:26-27), though their precise origin and nature are not fully revealed.
* **Spiritual beings:** Angels exist in a spiritual realm alongside the physical world, interacting with humans in various ways.

## Primary Role:

The primary role of angels in the Bible is to:

* **Communicate God's will:** Angels deliver pronouncements from God to individuals or nations.
* **Intercede for humanity:** Angels can mediate between God and humans, advocating for forgiveness and redemption (Hebrews 5:14).
* **Protect believers:** Angels are often assigned to protect individuals or communities (Psalm 34:7, Matthew 26:53).
* **Prepare the way:** Angels prepare the way for God's intervention in human history, often foreshadowing major events like the birth of Jesus (Matthew 2:13).

It's important to note that the Bible's portrayal of angels is nuanced and subject to interpretation. Different passages emphasize different aspects of their nature and purpose.
