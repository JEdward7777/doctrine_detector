* **"But God demonstrates his love for us in this: while we were still sinners, Christ died for us."** (Romans 5:8)

* **Intimacy:** The term suggests a close and personal relationship with God, marked by deep affection and acceptance.

**Theological Significance:**

* **God's love for his people:** The Bible teaches that God loves all people unconditionally, regardless of their actions or beliefs.
* **Christ's redemption:** The term "beloved" emphasizes that Christ died for God's beloved people, demonstrating the depth of God's love.
* **Divine election:** In some contexts, "beloved" can also refer to God's elect, those whom he has chosen for salvation.

**Interpretation:**

When the term "beloved" is used in the Bible, it conveys the profound and unconditional love that God has for his people. It highlights the special privilege and responsibility that comes with being loved by God.
