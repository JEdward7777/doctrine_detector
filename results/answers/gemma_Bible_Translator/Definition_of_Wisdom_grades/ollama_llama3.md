* Wisdom manifests in thoughtful deliberation, sound decisions, and responsible actions (Ecclesiastes 9:18).

**Key Characteristics of Biblical Wisdom:**

* **Fear of the Lord:** A deep reverence and love for God is the starting point of wisdom.
* **Humility:** Wisdom recognizes that true knowledge comes from God and acknowledges the limits of human understanding.
* **Integrity:** Wisdom avoids deception and embraces honesty and righteousness.
* **Compassion:** Wisdom understands the needs of others and seeks to help them.
* **Practicality:** Wisdom applies knowledge and skills to real-life situations.

* To be wise Christians, we must immerse ourselves in God's Word and apply its principles to our lives.
* We must humbly seek guidance from others who possess wisdom and experience.
* We must practice discerning between right and wrong and making informed decisions.
* We must demonstrate compassion and care for others.

* The proverbs of Solomon (Proverbs)
* The teachings of Jesus Christ (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John)
* The advice of Paul the Apostle (1 Corinthians, Ephesians)

Biblical wisdom is not merely intellectual knowledge but a practical application of God's Word and love in everyday life. By cultivating wisdom, Christians can live lives that honor God, make wise decisions, and demonstrate the fruit of the Spirit.
