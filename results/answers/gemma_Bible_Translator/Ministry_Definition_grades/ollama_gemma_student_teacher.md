**To 'minister' according to the Bible means to serve others in love and humility, offering spiritual nourishment and support.** 

The New Testament provides multiple examples of ministry, highlighting different aspects of this vital service.

**1. Compassionate Service:**

* "Love one another as I have loved you" (John 13:34).
* "Bear one another's burdens" (Galatians 6:2).
* "Rejoice with those who rejoice, weep with those who weep" (Romans 12:15).

**2. Spiritual Nourishment:**

* "Preach the word of God" (2 Timothy 4:2).
* "Teach, exhort, and encourage" (2 Timothy 4:5).
* "Disciple others" (Matthew 28:19).

**3. Practical Support:**

* "Practical works of love" (Galatians 6:10).
* "Help those in need" (James 2:14-17).
* "Sharing resources" (1 Timothy 6:18).

**4. Evangelism and Witnessing:**

* "Go and make disciples of all nations" (Matthew 28:19).
* "Witness for Christ" (1 Peter 3:15).
* "Preach the gospel" (2 Timothy 4:2).

**Key Principles of Biblical Ministry:**

* **Humility:** Ministry should be driven by love and a willingness to serve others, not by ego or personal gain.
* **Compassion:** Understanding and addressing the physical, emotional, and spiritual needs of others.
* **Service:** Offering time, talents, and resources to support and encourage others.
* **Love:** The foundation of all ministry, based on God's love for us.

**In summary, to 'minister' means to lovingly serve others, offering spiritual nourishment, practical support, and encouragement in their spiritual journey.**
