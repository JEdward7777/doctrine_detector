**1. Obedience to God's Word:**

* The Bible is the ultimate authority on Christian life and practice. 
* We are called to obey God's commands, which include loving God with all our heart, loving others as ourselves, and living in purity.


**2. Transformation through the Holy Spirit:**

* The Holy Spirit empowers believers for holy living. 
* Through the Holy Spirit's work, we are transformed into the image of Christ, developing Christ-like character and values.


**3. Purity of Heart and Actions:**

* A holy life involves purity in both heart and actions. 
* Our thoughts, words, and deeds should be motivated by God's love and characterized by integrity and righteousness.


**4. Self-Control and Moderation:**

* Christians are expected to exercise self-control over their appetites, emotions, and behaviors. 
* This includes avoiding excess, indulging in harmful substances, and compromising moral standards.


**5. Serving Others:**

* A holy life involves serving others. 
* Christ demonstrated love and compassion by serving others, and we are called to follow his example.


**6. Witnessing for Christ:**

* Our lives are a witness to the transforming power of God. 
* We are expected to live in such a way that others can see the love of Christ in our actions.


**7. Sanctity of Life:**

* Every human life is sacred to God. 
* We are called to respect and value life, upholding the dignity of every individual.


**8. Ongoing Growth and Development:**

* Living a holy life is an ongoing journey. 
* We are expected to continue learning and growing in our faith, striving to become more like Christ.

**9. Seeking God's Forgiveness:**

* None of us are perfect, and we will all make mistakes. 
* God's forgiveness and grace are essential for maintaining a holy life.


**10. Dependence on God:**

* Ultimately, living a holy life is dependent on God's grace and power. 
* We must rely on God's strength and guidance to live the lives he desires for us.
