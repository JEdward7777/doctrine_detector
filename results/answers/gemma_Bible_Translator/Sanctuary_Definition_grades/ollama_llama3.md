**In the Bible, the term "sanctuary" has two primary meanings:**

**1. Physical Sanctuary:**

* Refers to a designated place of refuge or safety, where individuals or objects are protected from harm, danger, or defilement.
* Examples include the Tabernacle and the Temple in the Old Testament, where God's presence was believed to reside.
* In the New Testament, the Church is often referred to as a sanctuary, as it is a safe haven for believers.

**2. Spiritual Sanctuary:**

* Represents a place of refuge for the soul in times of spiritual distress or temptation.
* This sanctuary is found in God himself, who offers peace, forgiveness, and strength to those who are struggling.
* The Psalms frequently speak of finding refuge in God's presence.

**Biblical references:**

* **Old Testament:**
    * "The Lord is my sanctuary, my refuge in times of trouble" (Psalm 9:14)
    * "He shall be my horn and my sanctuary" (Psalm 45:7)
* **New Testament:**
    * "But as he is the Son of Man, he was not seen as honorable by anyone in the world. He was despised and rejected by the rulers of this generation... **Therefore, the term "sanctuary" in the Bible encompasses both physical and spiritual concepts, emphasizing the idea of a safe and protective refuge in both physical and spiritual realms.**
