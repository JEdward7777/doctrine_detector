The biblical account of Jesus' temptations suggests he responded in several ways:

**1. Drawing on divine authority:**

* Jesus quoted scripture to counter Satan's arguments, demonstrating his reliance on God's Word as the ultimate authority. 
* This highlights Jesus' divine authority and his ability to resist temptation through God's Word.

**2. Spiritual preparation:**

* Jesus spent 40 days fasting and praying before facing Satan, demonstrating his spiritual preparation and dependence on God's strength.
* This highlights the importance of spiritual preparation in overcoming temptation.

**3. Firmness and declaration:**

* Jesus directly countered Satan's temptations with firm declarations, setting boundaries and refusing to be swayed. 
* This demonstrates Jesus' unwavering conviction and confidence in his identity as God's Son.

**4. Dependence on God:**

* Jesus referred to God as his source of strength and sustenance, highlighting his complete dependence on God in overcoming temptation.
* This underscores the vital role of God's power and presence in assisting believers to resist temptation.

**5. Humility and obedience:**

* Jesus demonstrated humility by acknowledging Satan's power and temptations, while remaining obedient to God's will. 
* This highlights the importance of humility and submission to God's authority in overcoming temptation.

**The ultimate response to Satan's temptations was Jesus' victory through his obedience to God and his reliance on divine authority.**
