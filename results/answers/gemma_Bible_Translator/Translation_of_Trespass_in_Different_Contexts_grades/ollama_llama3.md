# Question [Translation of Trespass in Different Contexts](../../../questions/Translation_of_Trespass_in_Different_Contexts.md)
What are the implications of translating 'trespass' as 'sin' or 'breaking the rule' when discussing inter-personal violations within a church community?

# Correct Answer

## Implications of Translating "Trespass"

**1. Theological Accuracy:**

- "Trespass" in the Bible specifically refers to violation of God's law or principles. 
- Translating it as "sin" conflates the concepts of sin as violation of God's law and sin as moral inadequacy. 
- This can lead to confusion and misunderstanding of God's forgiveness and justice.

**2. Cultural Context:**

- In some cultures, "trespass" implies an infringement on someone's territory or rights. 
- Translating it as "breaking the rule" emphasizes the violation of established norms, which may not capture the full weight of the biblical concept.

**3. Interpersonal Implications:**

- Using "trespass" emphasizes the violation of relationships and community. 
- This language can be more appropriate for addressing interpersonal conflicts within a church community, where healing and restoration are desired.

**4. Clarity for Non-Theological Audiences:**

- Translating "trespass" as "violation" or "breaking trust" can be clearer for non-theological audiences who may not be familiar with Christian theological terminology.

**Recommendations:**

- Use "trespass" when emphasizing violation of God's law or principles.
- Consider "violation" or "breach of trust" when discussing interpersonal violations within the church.
- Provide additional context to clarify the specific violation being referred to.

**Additional Considerations:**

- The specific translation choice should be consistent with the overall tone and audience of the communication.
- It is important to be sensitive to different cultural contexts and interpretations of terminology.
- The goal of translation is to communicate the truth of the Bible clearly and faithfully.
