**Biblical Significance:**

* **"Yahweh"** is a transliteration of the Hebrew word that literally means "I am the Lord."
* It is often translated as "LORD" or "Jehovah" in English Bibles.
* The name is most frequently used in the Psalms and prophetic books to refer to the one true God.

**Contextual Meaning:**

* In the Old Testament, God reveals himself as Yahweh to Abraham, Moses, and other patriarchs and prophets.
* The Israelites understood that Yahweh was their exclusive covenant partner, providing for their physical and spiritual needs.
* His faithfulness and unchanging love were central to the covenant relationship.

**Theological Significance:**

* The name 'Yahweh' highlights God's uniqueness and sovereignty as the one true God.
* It emphasizes God's covenant faithfulness to his people, even when they sinned and disobeyed him.
* The name reveals God's desire for a personal and intimate relationship with his people.

* God's covenantal faithfulness

* God's sovereignty and uniqueness as the one true God
