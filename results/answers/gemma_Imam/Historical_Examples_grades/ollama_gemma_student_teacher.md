- Built an altar to the Lord after the Ten Plagues (Exodus 17:1-6)

- Established altars and offered sacrifices at the Tabernacle (Leviticus 1:1-9)

**Significance of these Acts:**

**1. Manifestation of Faith:**
- Building an altar was a physical manifestation of the believers' faith in the unseen God.
- The act symbolized the offering of themselves and their possessions to God.

**2. Communication with God:**
- Altars served as a place for communication between the believers and God.
- Sacrifices and prayers offered on the altars were believed to reach God and influence his decisions.

**3. Covenant Renewal:**
- The act of building an altar often symbolized the renewal of a covenant between God and his people.
- The sacrifices offered represented the believers' commitment to obey God's laws and live in accordance with his will.

**4. Symbols of Sacrifice and Redemption:**
- The sacrifices offered on the altars symbolized the need for redemption from sin.
- The shedding of blood was seen as a cover for sin and a representation of God's forgiveness.

**5. Expression of Gratitude:**
- Building altars could also be a way to express gratitude for God's blessings.
- The sacrifices offered would symbolize the believers' appreciation for God's mercy and protection.
