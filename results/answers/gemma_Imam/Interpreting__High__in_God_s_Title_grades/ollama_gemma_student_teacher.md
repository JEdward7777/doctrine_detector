## Question [Interpreting 'High' in God's Title](../../../questions/Interpreting__High__in_God_s_Title.md)
How should we interpret the term 'high' in the title 'Most High' when considering God's nature?

## Correct Answer
'High' refers to greatness and authority, not to physical height, emphasizing God's supremacy over everything.

## Stated Concern
It is important to avoid misconceptions about God's nature and to understand the metaphorical use of descriptive titles.

**In the Islamic context, the term "high" in "Most High" refers to God's profound greatness, majestic power, and unparalleled sovereignty.**

**The Arabic word "al-alim" (Most High), used in Islamic theology, implies:**

* **Extreme loftiness:** God is far above and beyond any human conception or comparison.
* **Omnipotence:** God's power is unlimited and encompasses all things.
* **Sublime majesty:** God's glory and splendor are beyond human comprehension.

**The concept of God's "height" or "loftiness" is emphasized in various Islamic sources:**

* The Quran: "Say, 'He is Allah, the One, the Ever-Existent, the Self-Subsisting, the Most High (al-alim).'" (Quran 2:255)
* Hadith: The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said, "Allah is al-alimul-alim (the Most High of the Most High)."

**When interpreting the term "high" in the title "Most High," Muslims should understand it as:**

* A declaration of God's unmatched greatness and sovereignty.
* A reminder of God's absolute power and control over all things.
* An invitation to awe and humility before God's immense glory.

**The concept of God's "Most Highness" emphasizes:**

* God's uniqueness and singularity.
* His independence from creation.
* His perfect and eternal nature.
