**Both 'propitiation' and 'atonement' are closely related concepts in Islamic theology, but with slight differences:**

**Propitiation:**

* Refers to actions or sacrifices made to appease or conciliate a deity.
* Implies a sense of imbalance or tension between the deity and humanity.
* Often involves physical offerings or rituals.

**Atonement:**

* Deals with the reconciliation or forgiveness of sins.
* Emphasizes the removal of the penalty or punishment for wrongdoing.
* Often involves spiritual or moral transformation alongside any physical atonment.

**In the Islamic context:**

* Both terms are used to describe the process of seeking forgiveness and reconciliation with Allah.
* **Propitiation** often focuses on physical actions like prayer, charity, or fasting.
* **Atonement** emphasizes the need for sincere repentance, good deeds, and the fulfillment of religious obligations.

**Both concepts ultimately aim to:**

* Restore harmony and balance with Allah.
* Remove the barrier of sin between humanity and Allah.
* Obtain forgiveness and redemption.

**The Islamic concept of 'istighfar' (seeking forgiveness) encompasses both propitiation and atonement:**

* **Propitiation:** Making physical offerings or performing rituals as a sign of remorse and humility.
* **Atonement:** Genuine repentance, changing one's behavior, and fulfilling religious obligations.

**Therefore, in Islamic terms, 'propitiation' is a part of 'atonement,' which is the comprehensive process of seeking forgiveness and reconciliation with Allah.**
