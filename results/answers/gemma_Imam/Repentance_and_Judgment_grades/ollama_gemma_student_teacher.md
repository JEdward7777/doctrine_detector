**Biblical Perspective on Repentance and God's Wrath:**

The Bible teaches a profound connection between repentance and God's wrath.

**1. Repentance is God's Remedy for Wrath:**

- Throughout Scripture, God's wrath is directed towards sin and rebellion.
- Repentance is presented as the antidote to God's wrath.
- The prophet Isaiah declares, "He will forgive our sins and remember our trespasses no more" (Isaiah 43:25).

**2. God's Wrath is Not Eternal:**

- God's wrath is not an eternal punishment but a temporary discipline.
- The apostle Paul writes, "For the wrath of God is revealed from heaven to punish all the sins and lawlessness of men" (Romans 1:18).
- However, God's wrath is ultimately resolved in love and mercy.

**3. True Repentance Involves Change of Heart:**

- Genuine repentance involves a complete change of heart and life.
- It includes acknowledging sin, seeking forgiveness, and turning away from evil.
- The prophet Ezekiel emphasizes, "Repent, and be saved... Turn away from all your transgressions" (Ezekiel 33:13).

**4. God's Wrath is a Call to Repentance:**

- God's wrath can be a catalyst for repentance.
- The apostle James writes, "Grace and peace to you from God our Father and the Lord Jesus Christ. Consider it all a great gift, dear friends, not something to be grumbled about" (James 1:14).

**5. God's Mercy Follows Repentance:**

- When we repent of our sins, God's mercy and forgiveness are available to us.
- The prophet Jeremiah declares, "I will forgive their sins and will remember them no more" (Jeremiah 31:34).

**Conclusion:**

The Bible teaches that repentance is an essential element in experiencing God's forgiveness and avoiding his wrath. True repentance involves sincere acknowledgment of sin, heartfelt apology, and a radical change of life.
