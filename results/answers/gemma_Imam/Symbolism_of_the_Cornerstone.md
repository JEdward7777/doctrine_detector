**The metaphor of the cornerstone illustrates the essential and foundational relationship between Jesus and His followers in several ways:**

**1. Jesus as the Foundation:**

* Just as a cornerstone provides structural support and stability to a building, Jesus is the ultimate foundation of faith and spiritual growth for His followers.
* His teachings and example become the bedrock upon which believers build their lives and actions.

**2. Unity and Interconnection:**

* A cornerstone connects different parts of a building, ensuring unity and coherence. Similarly, Jesus binds His followers together as a community.
* He fosters a sense of belonging and interdependence among His followers, who are all interconnected through their faith in Him.

**3. Authority and Guidance:**

* The cornerstone provides a point of reference and authority for builders. Jesus is the ultimate authority and guide for His followers, providing direction and wisdom in their faith journey.
* His teachings and principles establish the foundation for moral and spiritual behavior.

**4. Support and Protection:**

* A cornerstone protects the building from collapse and harm. Jesus offers support and protection to His followers, shielding them from spiritual and worldly challenges.
* He stands with them in times of adversity and provides solace and guidance when needed.

**5. Growth and Development:**

* As a cornerstone helps a building grow and expand, Jesus' relationship with His followers fosters spiritual growth and development.
* His teachings and guidance inspire believers to reach their full potential in their faith.

**6. Loyalty and Commitment:**

* A cornerstone symbolizes unwavering loyalty and commitment. Jesus' followers are expected to be loyal and committed to Him, building their lives and actions in accordance with His teachings.
* This loyalty is demonstrated through obedience, devotion, and service.
