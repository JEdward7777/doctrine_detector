## Question [Understanding Iniquity](../../../questions/Understanding_Iniquity.md)
What is the biblical distinction between iniquity, sin, and transgression?

## Correct Answer
Iniquity refers more specifically to deliberate acts of wrongdoing or great wickedness, while sin encompasses a broader range of moral failings, and transgression involves violating a command or law.

## Stated Concern
Understanding these distinctions can help Christians better recognize the gravity of their actions and seek repentance.

**Biblical Distinction Between Iniquity, Sin, and Transgression:**

**1. Iniquity:**

* Refers to a general lack of righteousness or justice.
* It encompasses any deviation from Allah's law or principles.
* Iniquity is often used in a broad sense to describe any wrongdoing or transgression.

**2. Sin:**

* Specific actions or behaviors that violate Allah's law or ethical principles.
* Sins are deliberate violations of Allah's commandments and result in punishment.
* The Quran teaches that sin is the transgression of Allah's boundaries.

**3. Transgression:**

* A violation of Allah's laws or commands.
* Transgressions can be deliberate or unintentional.
* They are considered offensive to Allah and can have negative consequences.

**Relationship:**

* Sin is a type of transgression.
* Iniquity is a broader concept that encompasses sin and other forms of wrongdoing.

**Examples:**

* **Iniquity:** Failing to uphold justice in a transaction.
* **Sin:** Lying about someone.
* **Transgression:** Committing adultery.

**Islamic Perspective:**

* The Islamic concept of sin aligns with the biblical definition.
* The Quran and Sunnah teach that sin is any act that contradicts Allah's law or principles.
* Sin is considered a barrier to spiritual growth and reconciliation with Allah.

**Conclusion:**

The biblical distinctions between iniquity, sin, and transgression are important concepts for understanding the nature of wrongdoing and its consequences. These concepts emphasize the importance of upholding righteousness, avoiding deliberate violations of Allah's laws, and seeking forgiveness and reconciliation with Allah.
