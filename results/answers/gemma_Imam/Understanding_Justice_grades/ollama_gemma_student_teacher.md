## Question [Understanding Justice](../../../questions/Understanding_Justice.md)
What does it mean to act justly according to God's laws?

## Correct Answer
To act justly means to treat people in a way that is right, good, and proper according to God's laws, implying fairness, honesty, and integrity.

## Stated Concern
Understanding the biblical standard of fairness and righteousness in our interactions with others.

## [Answer](../Understanding_Justice.md) by [gemma Imam](../../../answering_models/gemma_Imam.md)
**Acting justly according to God's laws means:**

**1. Adherence to Divine Commands:**

* Upholding Islamic legal principles (sharia) in daily life.
* Performing obligatory prayers, fasting, charity, and pilgrimage.
* Abstaining from prohibited actions such as lying, stealing, and transgressing bounds.


**2. Fairness and Balance:**

* Treating others with equity and impartiality.
* Rendering fair judgments and decisions in disputes.
* Balancing personal interests with the rights of others.


**3. Accountability and Responsibility:**

* Taking ownership of our actions and their consequences.
* Judging ourselves justly and striving to improve.
* Seeking accountability from others when necessary.


**4. Social Justice:**

* Protecting the rights of the vulnerable and marginalized.
* Contributing to the welfare of society.
* Promoting justice and fairness in economic, social, and political systems.


**5. Internal Justice:**

* Maintaining integrity and honesty in our thoughts and actions.
* Cultivating empathy and compassion for others.
* Balancing our material pursuits with spiritual values.


**The Quran emphasizes the importance of justice:**

> "Indeed, God commands justice, and He instructs you to be patient and to obey the believers." (Quran 60:8)

**The Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) also taught:**

> "The most perfect among you is the one who is most just."

**Therefore, acting justly is an integral aspect of fulfilling God's laws and living a fulfilling life according to Islamic principles.**
