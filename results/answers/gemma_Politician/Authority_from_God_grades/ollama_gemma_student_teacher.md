**Divine authority** is a theological concept that refers to the ultimate source of power and legitimacy in the life of a believer. It is the belief that God or a divine being has established certain principles, laws, and authority that are binding on believers.

**Significance in the life of a believer:**

* **Moral guidance:** Divine authority provides a moral framework for believers' actions and decisions.
* **Ethical behavior:** It emphasizes the importance of treating others with respect and integrity.
* **Spiritual authority:** Believers often seek guidance from religious texts, teachings, and leaders who have divine authority.
* **Accountability:** The belief in divine authority implies accountability for one's actions before a higher power.
* **Identity and purpose:** Divine authority can provide a sense of identity and purpose in life.

**Note:** The concept of divine authority is a religious belief and is not a political or secular concept.
