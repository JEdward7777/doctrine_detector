## Question [Judgment of Others](../../../questions/Judgment_of_Others.md)
What does Jesus teach us about pointing out the faults of others in relation to our own faults?

## Correct Answer
Jesus teaches us to first remove the plank from our own eye before pointing out the speck in someone else's eye, reminding us to practice humility and self-reflection (Luke 6:41-42).

## Stated Concern
Criticizing others while ignoring our own faults can reflect hypocrisy and diminish our witness as Christians.

## [Answer](../Judgment_of_Others.md) by [gemma Politician](../../../answering_models/gemma_Politician.md)
**Jesus' teachings on pointing out the faults of others in relation to our own faults:**

* **Practice radical humility:**
    - Jesus himself modeled radical humility, focusing on serving others and meeting their needs rather than on self-promotion or judgment.
    - We should examine our own hearts before pointing fingers at others.


* **Consider our own sins:**
    - Before we point out the speck in someone else's eye, we must ensure we don't have a log in our own eye. 
    - The Golden Rule teaches us to treat others as we would like to be treated.


* **Use gentle words:**
    - Our words can either build up or tear down others. 
    - We should speak with kindness, respect, and sensitivity.


* **Focus on transformation:**
    - Jesus emphasized the importance of helping others grow and change. 
    - Our goal should be to encourage and support others in their journey, not to condemn them.


* **Avoid confrontation:**
    - Jesus often avoided direct confrontation, choosing instead to use parables and indirect methods to convey his message. 
    - We should consider the context and timing of our conversations and avoid unnecessary conflict.


* **Model forgiveness:**
    - Jesus freely forgave others of their sins, setting an example for us to follow. 
    - If we want to point out the faults of others, we must be willing to forgive them as well.
