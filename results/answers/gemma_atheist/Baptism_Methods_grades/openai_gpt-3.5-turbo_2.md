## Different Methods of Baptism Mentioned in the Bible:

**1. Immersion:**

* Most widely practiced method. 
* Represents washing away of sin and entering new life in Christ. 
* Examples: Matthew 3:13, Acts 8:38.


**2. Sprinkling:**

* Used primarily in Reformed traditions.
* Represents washing the external body as a symbol of cleansing the heart. 
* Examples: 1 Corinthians 12:13, Revelation 1:17.


**3. Pouring:**

* Less common method. 
* Represents the pouring out of sins and receiving the Holy Spirit. 
* Examples: James 5:14-15.


**4. Washing with Water:**

* Not explicitly mentioned in the Bible, but sometimes associated with baptism in early church history. 
* Represents physical cleansing alongside spiritual rebirth.


## Interpretations in Various Christian Contexts:

**1. Evangelical/Pentecostal:**

* Emphasis on rebirth through water immersion.
* Seen as an essential step for salvation.
* Often linked with receiving the Holy Spirit.


**2. Catholic/Orthodox:**

* Emphasis on baptism as a sacrament, signifying forgiveness of sins and entrance into the Church.
* Uses immersion or pouring.
* Considered essential for salvation.


**3. Protestant Denominations:**

* Views baptism as a symbolic representation of spiritual rebirth.
* Emphasis on the internal transformation rather than external ritual.
* May use immersion, sprinkling, or pouring.


**4. Non-Traditional/Charismatic Movements:**

* Focus on the spiritual significance of baptism.
