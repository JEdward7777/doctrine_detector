## Jesus' teachings on pointing out the faults of others in relation to our own faults:

**1. Practice self-awareness:**

- "Judge not, that you be not judged" (Matthew 7:1).
- "Examine yourself, that you may have something to offer" (2 Corinthians 13:5).

**2. Focus on your own shortcomings first:**

- "Hypocrite! First remove the plank from your own eye, and then you will see clearly to remove the speck from your brother's eye" (Matthew 7:3-5).
- "Why do you see the speck in your brother's eye, but fail to notice the plank in your own eye?" (Matthew 7:4).

**3. Communicate with humility and love:**

- "A gentle answer turns away wrath, but a harsh word stirs up anger" (Proverbs 15:1).
- "Love your neighbor as yourself" (Matthew 22:39).

**4. Be mindful of motives:**

- "Do not speak evil of anyone" (Colossians 4:6).
- "Let your speech be always gracious, seasoned with salt" (Colossians 4:6).

**5. Aim for reconciliation:**

- "If you bring your gift to the altar and there you remember that your brother has something against you, leave your gift there at the altar" (Matthew 6:15).
- "Make peace with your brother, and be reconciled" (Matthew 5:23).

**Additional insights:**

- Jesus' teachings advocate for a humble and compassionate approach to addressing the faults of others. 
- The focus should be on personal growth and transformation rather than judgment or condemnation.
- Open communication and willingness to reconcile are key elements in Jesus' model for dealing with conflict.
