**Jesus' use of parables served multiple purposes in his teachings:**

**1. Cultural Accessibility:**

* Parables were accessible to the common people of first-century Judea, who were largely illiterate and unfamiliar with complex theological concepts.
* They used familiar objects and situations to illustrate spiritual truths in ways that were relatable to their daily lives.

**2. Simplicity and Clarity:**

* Parables were simple and easy to understand, even for those with little formal education.
* Their use of exaggeration and symbolism made them memorable and impactful.

**3. Transcending Cultural Boundaries:**

* Parables transcended cultural boundaries and could be understood by people from different backgrounds and languages.
* They focused on universal themes such as love, forgiveness, and redemption.

**4. Engaging Attention:**

* The use of parables helped to engage people's attention and make his teachings more memorable.
* The unexpected comparisons and humorous elements of parables made them thought-provoking and entertaining.

**5. Illustrating Spiritual Truths:**

* Parables served as illustrations of spiritual truths and ethical values.
* They revealed the nature of God, human sinfulness, and the need for redemption.

**6. Challenging Assumptions:**

* Parables often challenged the assumptions of the listeners.
* They showed that true spiritual understanding required a willingness to rethink conventional wisdom.

**7. Promoting Reflection:**

* Parables invited listeners to reflect on their own lives and actions.
* They posed questions about their values, beliefs, and priorities.
