**Testing** and **temptation** are often confused or conflated in biblical interpretations. However, there are distinct differences between the two concepts.

**Testing:**

* Refers to the process of being subjected to trials, challenges, or difficulties.
* Emphasizes the strengthening and refining of an individual through adversity.
* Focuses on God's sovereignty and purpose in allowing these experiences.
* Often involves external forces beyond an individual's control.

**Temptation:**

* Deals with the internal struggle to resist desires, cravings, or temptations.
* Emphasis on individual choices and decisions.
* Focuses on the conflict between what is desired and what is known to be right.
* Primarily involves internal factors and temptations inherent in human nature.

**Key Differences:**

* **Origin:** Testing originates from God's decree, while temptation arises from within an individual.
* **Nature:** Testing involves external forces, while temptation is an internal struggle.
* **Purpose:** Testing leads to spiritual growth, while temptation leads to potential moral compromise.
