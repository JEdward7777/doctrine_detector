## Distinctions between Biblical and worldly glory:

**1. Motivation:**

* **Biblical:** Primarily focuses on praising God for his inherent worth and attributes, emphasizing his justice, mercy, and sovereignty. 
* **Worldly:** Often driven by self-interest, seeking recognition for achievements, beauty, or power.

**2. Source:**

* **Biblical:** Glorification originates from God himself, not from human actions or accomplishments. 
* **Worldly:** Glorification comes from external validation through human applause, social media likes, or material possessions.

**3. Focus:**

* **Biblical:** God's glory transcends human comprehension and focuses on his character and attributes. 
* **Worldly:** Emphasis is placed on outward displays of success, often neglecting internal growth or social responsibility.

**4. Duration:**

* **Biblical:** Glorification is eternal and centered on God's character, which remains constant regardless of human actions. 
* **Worldly:** Glory is temporary and often fleeting, lasting only as long as the achievement or possession exists.

**5. Consequences:**

* **Biblical:** Glorifying God aligns with living a life of obedience and love for others. 
* **Worldly:** Chasing worldly glory can lead to pride, envy, and dissatisfaction.
