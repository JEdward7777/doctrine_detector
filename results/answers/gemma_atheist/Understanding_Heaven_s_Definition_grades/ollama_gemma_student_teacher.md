## Biblical Definition of Heaven

The biblical definition of "heaven" is multifaceted and transcends a purely physical interpretation. It encompasses:

**1. Literal Heaven:**
- Literal ascension of the righteous to a physical realm above the earth. 
- Primarily referenced in Old Testament prophecies and the Book of Revelation.

**2. Spiritual Realm:**
- Represents the dwelling place of God and the spirits of the redeemed.
- Emphasized in New Testament writings like Paul's letters and the Gospel accounts.

**3. Heavenly Union:**
- Represents a state of perfect peace, joy, and communion with God.
- Emphasized in passages like Psalm 37:11 and John 14:23.


## Difference from 'Sky'

While both terms share the word "heaven," they carry distinct meanings:

**Sky:**
- Refers to the physical expanse of the atmosphere above the earth.
- Primarily used in meteorological or geographical contexts.

**Heaven:**
- Represents a spiritual realm beyond the physical sky.
- Emphasizes the transcendent and eternal nature of the afterlife.


**Key differences:**

- **Nature:** Sky is a physical phenomenon, while heaven is a spiritual realm.
- **Content:** Sky refers to the physical space above, while heaven represents a state of being for the righteous.
- **Permanence:** Sky is temporary, while heaven is eternal.
