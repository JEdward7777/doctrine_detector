2. **Initiation into the Community of Faith**: Acts 2:41 highlights that baptism is a means of initiation into the Christian community. Thus, baptism is understood as an act of obedience to Christ’s directive, reinforcing the believer's commitment to live according to His teachings.

4. **Covenantal Sign**: Baptism is often viewed as a sign of the new covenant established by Jesus. 5. **Assurance of Spiritual Transformation**: The act of baptism represents not only the washing away of sins but also the believer’s spiritual renewal and the reception of the Holy Spirit, as seen in passages like Acts 2:38. It is seen as an important step in the process of salvation, reflecting an inner change that has occurred.

6. **Public Testimony**: Baptism is an outward symbol of an inward transformation and faith, serving as a public testimony to the individual's commitment to follow Christ. This aspect can be observed throughout the New Testament, as baptized individuals often share their stories of faith, encouraging others and spreading the message of Christianity.

In summary, baptism in the New Testament is a multifaceted act that functions as a means of grace, an expression of faith, an entry into the Christian community, and a point of obedience to Christ’s teachings.
