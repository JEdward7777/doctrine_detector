This figure is not divine but a human leader who fulfills specific prophetic criteria outlined in the Hebrew Scriptures. The Jewish expectation emphasizes communal restoration and adherence to the Law.

2. **Christianity**: Christians view Jesus Christ as the Messiah (Christos in Greek), believing him to be the fulfillment of the Old Testament prophecies. 3. **Islam**: In Islam, Jesus (Isa) is also recognized as the Messiah, but he is not the son of God, as per Islamic teachings. This expectation highlights a future conflict between good and evil rather than a personal savior for individual believers.

The Baha'i view emphasizes the unity of all religions and the unfolding of God's plan through various messianic figures throughout history.

5. **Other Traditions**: Various other religious and cultural traditions have their own interpretations of messianic figures, often tied to local myths or historical contexts. These figures might represent hope for social justice, national revival, or spiritual awakening.

In summary, while the term "Messiah" conveys the idea of an awaited savior or leader, the specific attributes and roles assigned to this figure diverge across different faiths.
