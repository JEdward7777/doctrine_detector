In the biblical Book of Acts, particularly in Acts 2, the Apostle Peter addresses the crowd after Pentecost. He emphasizes their role in the crucifixion of Jesus by directly stating that they were complicit in the act. He points out that Jesus, whom they had crucified, was delivered up according to God's predetermined plan and foreknowledge. Peter stresses that despite the divine plan, their actions in rejecting and killing Jesus constituted a grave wrongdoing.

Peter's address serves multiple purposes: it acknowledges the crowd's guilt while also framing the event within the context of God's overarching plan for salvation. He calls for repentance, urging them to turn away from their actions and embrace the message of Christ, thus emphasizing both accountability and the possibility of redemption.
