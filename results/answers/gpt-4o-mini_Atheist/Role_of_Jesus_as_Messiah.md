1. **Present Reality**: Jesus often spoke of the Kingdom in terms of it being at hand or already present among his followers (e.g., Luke 17:21). This suggests that the Kingdom is not merely a future event, but a current reality that is experienced through the teachings, actions, and community of believers. As the Messiah, Jesus embodies the values of this Kingdom: love, justice, mercy, and reconciliation. His role involves not only proclaiming the Kingdom but also exemplifying its principles, thereby inviting people to live according to its ethics.

2. **Future Hope**: At the same time, the Kingdom is seen as a future fulfillment where God's reign will be fully realized, often characterized by peace, justice, and the restoration of creation. In Jewish messianic expectations, this future aspect includes the idea of a political and spiritual leader who would liberate Israel and establish God's rule. Jesus is seen by his followers as fulfilling this expectation, albeit in ways that diverged from conventional interpretations of a political Messiah. His crucifixion and resurrection are viewed as transformative events that inaugurate this Kingdom, aligning with the idea of a suffering servant rather than a conquering king.

3. **Ethical Implications**: The Kingdom of God underlines a radical re-envisioning of societal norms. Jesus' message challenges existing structures of power and invites a reorientation towards the marginalized and oppressed. His role as the Messiah thus involves bringing forth a new understanding of leadership and community, emphasizing servanthood over dominion.

In sum, the relationship between the Kingdom and Jesus' role as the Messiah encompasses both a present embodiment of God's reign through his ministry and teachings, and a future hope for complete restoration and justice.
