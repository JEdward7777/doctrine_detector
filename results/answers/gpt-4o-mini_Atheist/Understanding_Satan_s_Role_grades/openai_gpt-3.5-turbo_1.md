In various biblical texts, Satan is depicted as a being who challenges the faith of humans, tempts them to sin, and acts as an accuser in the heavenly court.

The role of Satan evolves throughout the scriptures, appearing in the Old Testament as a figure who tests the faith of individuals, as seen in the Book of Job, where he questions Job's righteousness. In the New Testament, Satan is more explicitly associated with temptation and evil, particularly in the accounts of the temptation of Jesus and the portrayal of a cosmic struggle between good and evil.

Satan is also connected to the idea of deception, as he is often described as a liar and a source of falsehood. This adversarial role has led to the broader concept of spiritual warfare within Christian theology, where believers are called to resist Satan's influence and remain faithful to God. Therefore, in summary, "Satan" symbolizes opposition to divine will, challenges to faith, and the presence of evil in the world.
