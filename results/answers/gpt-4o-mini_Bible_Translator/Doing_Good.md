2. **Service and Almsgiving**: "Doing good to" often manifests in acts of service, charity, and generosity. This includes helping those in need, giving to the poor, participating in community service, and being a source of support for those who are struggling (Galatians 6:9-10).

5. **Building Community**: Doing good helps to cultivate a spirit of unity and fellowship within the body of Christ, promoting care and accountability among believers (Ephesians 4:32).

In summary, "do good to" signifies not just a passive sentiment but an active, intentional expression of love and good deeds influenced by one's faith in Christ. It calls for engagement in actions that uplift and support others as a manifestation of Christian love and discipleship.
