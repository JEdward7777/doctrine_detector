Additionally, Hebrews 7:26-27 highlights that Jesus does not need to offer sacrifices for His own sins, as human priests do, because He is sinless. However, the New Testament teaches that Jesus' sacrifice was perfect and sufficient to atone for all sin (Hebrews 9:11-14). With His death and resurrection, the need for further sacrificial systems has been fulfilled, and believers are invited to approach God directly through Christ (Ephesians 2:18, Hebrews 10:19-22).

In summary, Jesus is referred to as our High Priest, which means that He has established a new covenant in which His once-for-all sacrifice renders the continuous sacrificial offerings of human priests unnecessary. This theology signifies a shift from the Old Covenant's sacrificial system to a new understanding of atonement and communion with God through Jesus Christ.
