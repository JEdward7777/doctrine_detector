2. **Redemption from Sin**: In Christian theology, humanity is viewed as being in bondage to sin and under the penalty of death, as articulated in Romans 6:23. 3. **Substitutionary Atonement**: This idea is closely linked to the doctrine of substitutionary atonement, which posits that Jesus died in the place of sinners, taking upon Himself the punishment they deserved. This sacrificial act is believed to satisfy divine justice, allowing God to extend grace and forgiveness to those who believe in Him.

4. **Fulfillment of Prophecy**: The notion of ransom also ties back to Old Testament sacrificial practices where animals were offered to atone for sins. Jesus is viewed as the ultimate fulfillment of these sacrifices, fulfilling prophetic scriptures about the Messiah bearing the sins of many (Isaiah 53:5-6).

5. **Inclusion of All People**: The term signifies not just individual redemption but also serves as a universal offer of salvation. Jesus’ ransom is extended to all humanity, indicating that anyone who believes in Him can receive forgiveness and eternal life (John 3:16).

6. **Hope of Liberation**: Finally, the idea of Jesus as a ransom carries with it a message of hope and liberation—not only from sin but also from the consequences of sin, such as guilt and estrangement from God. It speaks to God's love and desire to restore His creation.
