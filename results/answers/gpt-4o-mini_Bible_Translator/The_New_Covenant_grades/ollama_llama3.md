In the New Covenant, the promise that God would write His law on the people's hearts signifies a profound transformation in the relationship between God and His people. This concept is rooted in several passages in the Bible, most notably Jeremiah 31:31-33, where God promises a new covenant with His people that differs from the old covenant made with their ancestors. 

When God states that He would write His law on their hearts, it indicates several key theological truths:

1. **Internalization of God's Will**: Unlike the old covenant, which was primarily focused on external laws and regulations, the New Covenant emphasizes an internal change. God’s law becomes part of the believer's inner being, shaping their thoughts, desires, and actions. This internalization implies that obedience to God's commandments will come from a transformed heart rather than mere obligation.

2. **Intimacy of Relationship**: The New Covenant highlights a more personal and relational dynamic between God and His people. Instead of an external set of rules to follow, believers are brought into a close relationship with God where His desires and intentions are understood and embraced from within.

3. **Empowerment through the Holy Spirit**: The New Covenant is made possible through the work of the Holy Spirit, who empowers believers to live out God's commandments. As seen in the New Testament, particularly in passages like Acts 2 (the coming of the Holy Spirit at Pentecost) and Romans 8 (the role of the Spirit in the believer’s life), it is the Holy Spirit who writes God’s truths on the hearts of believers, enabling them to live righteously.

4. **Universal Access**: The New Covenant extends beyond the nation of Israel to include all believers (see Galatians 3:28-29). This universality suggests that the laws of God are not limited to a specific people or geographic location but are applicable to all who have faith in Christ. 

5. **Transformation of Lives**: The writing of God’s law on the heart signifies a transformative work in the believer’s life, producing fruit such as love, joy, and peace that reflects the character of Christ (Galatians 5:22-23). This transformation leads to a genuine desire to follow God's ways out of love rather than just duty.

In summary, the idea of God writing His law on His people’s hearts in the New Covenant signifies a deep, personal, and transformative relationship with God, characterized by an internalized understanding of His will, empowerment through the Holy Spirit, and a life lived in faithful obedience to His commands.
