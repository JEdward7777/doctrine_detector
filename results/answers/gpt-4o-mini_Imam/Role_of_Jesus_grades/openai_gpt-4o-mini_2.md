2. **Atonement**: One of the most critical aspects of Jesus' role is His atoning sacrifice. It is taught that through His crucifixion, Jesus paid the penalty for the sins of humanity (Isaiah 53:5, Romans 5:8). This sacrifice is seen as fulfilling the requirements of justice while offering mercy.

3. **Resurrection**: The resurrection of Jesus is viewed as a definitive victory over sin and death (1 Corinthians 15:55-57). It is believed to confirm His divine nature and the truth of His teachings.

4. **Faith and Grace**: Salvation is often described in terms of faith. Christians believe that through faith in Jesus Christ and His redemptive work, believers receive God's grace (Ephesians 2:8-9). This grace is a gift that cannot be earned through human effort.

5. **Mediator**: Jesus is viewed as the mediator between God and humanity (1 Timothy 2:5). His role as intercessor means He advocates on behalf of believers before God.

6. **Eternal Life**: Jesus promises eternal life to those who believe in Him (John 3:16). He teaches that belief in Him leads to spiritual rebirth and reconciliation with God.
