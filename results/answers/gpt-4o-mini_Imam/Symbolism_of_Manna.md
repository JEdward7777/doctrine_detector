When we reflect on the story of manna, it serves as a reminder that Allah is the Sustainer (Ar-Razzaq) who provides for His creation in ways that may be beyond our understanding. It highlights the idea of reliance on Allah for our needs and the belief that He will provide for us in difficult times.

Moreover, manna can also symbolize the importance of gratitude and reliance on God. Just as the Israelites were instructed to gather only what they needed for each day and not to hoard, Muslims are reminded to trust in Allah's provision and express gratitude for the blessings they receive. It signifies the relationship between divine provision and human responsibility, emphasizing the need for faith and trust in God's wisdom.
