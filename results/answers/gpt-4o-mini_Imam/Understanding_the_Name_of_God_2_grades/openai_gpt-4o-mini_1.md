In Jewish tradition, 'Yahweh' represents God's relationship with His people, Israel, and is closely associated with the covenant that God made with them. It emphasizes His role as the creator and sustainer of all that exists, as well as His faithfulness to His promises.

The name 'Yahweh' also highlights the concept of God as active in human history, particularly in the events of creation, the Exodus from Egypt, and throughout the history of Israel. It is important to note that out of respect and reverence, many Jews avoid pronouncing 'Yahweh' and instead refer to Him as "Adonai" (my Lord) or "HaShem" (the Name).
