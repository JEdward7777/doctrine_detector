2. **Omniscience**: God possesses complete knowledge and understanding of all things, both past and future (Psalm 139:1-4).

3. **Omnibenevolence**: God is inherently good and loving. This characteristic is especially emphasized in the New Testament (1 John 4:8).

4. **Omnipresence**: God is present everywhere simultaneously (Psalm 139:7-10).

5. **Holiness**: God is completely separate from sin and embodies perfect moral purity (Isaiah 6:3).

6. **Justice**: God is just and ensures that righteousness prevails; He delivers justice to the oppressed (Psalm 89:14).

7. **Mercy and Grace**: God shows compassion and extends grace to humanity, offering forgiveness (Ephesians 2:4-5).

8. **Immutability**: God does not change; His character and promises remain constant (Malachi 3:6).

9. **Relational Nature**: The Bible portrays God as desiring a personal relationship with humanity, as seen through the covenant relationships and the life of Jesus (John 3:16).

10. **Trinitarian View**: In Christian theology, God is understood as a Trinity—Father, Son, and Holy Spirit—each fully and equally God, yet distinct in their roles.
