2. **Understanding and Discernment**: Wisdom involves understanding the nature of God and His creation, as well as discerning right from wrong. 3. **Christ as the Source of Wisdom**: In 1 Corinthians 1:24, Paul refers to Christ as "the power of God and the wisdom of God." James 3:13 asks, "Who is wise and understanding among you? Let them show it by their good life, by deeds done in the humility that comes from wisdom." This highlights that wisdom manifests in actions and ethical behavior.

5. **Counsel and Guidance**: Proverbs 11:14 states, "Where there is no counsel, the people fall; but in the multitude of counselors, there is safety." Here, wisdom is associated with seeking advice and guidance, suggesting that wise individuals value the input of others and the community.

6. **Enduring Character**: Biblically, wisdom is also tied to character and conduct. Wise individuals are often portrayed as having traits such as humility, patience, and integrity. Proverbs 3:13-18 describes the blessings that come from finding wisdom and emphasizes its preciousness.

7. **Eternal Perspective**: Wisdom involves recognizing the temporality of earthly life compared to the eternal nature of spiritual life. Colossians 3:2 advises believers to "Set your minds on things that are above, not on things that are on earth." This reflects a wise perspective that values eternal truths over temporal concerns.
