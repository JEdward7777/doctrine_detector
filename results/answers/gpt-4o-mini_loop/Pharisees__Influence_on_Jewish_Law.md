1. **Conflict over Interpretation of the Law**: Jesus frequently challenged the Pharisees' interpretations of the Law. For instance, in Matthew 5:21-48, He reinterprets laws concerning murder, adultery, and oaths, emphasizing the spirit of the Law over the letter. This approach posed a direct challenge to the Pharisees' authority and understanding, leading to significant conflict.

In Matthew 12:7, He declares, “I desire mercy, not sacrifice.” This message resonated with early Christians who sought to embody Jesus’ teachings on love, mercy, and grace, rather than strict legalism.

3. **View of Righteousness**: The Pharisees believed that righteousness was attained through adherence to the Law. In contrast, Jesus taught that true righteousness comes from a transformed heart and a personal relationship with God. This difference underpinned significant theological disputes and contributed to the growing rift between Jesus’ followers and the Pharisaic traditions.

4. **Reactions to Jesus' Ministry**: The Pharisees are depicted in the Gospels as often opposing Jesus’ ministry. They questioned His authority (Matthew 21:23) and confronted Him regarding actions like healing on the Sabbath (Mark 3:1-6). Their opposition played a crucial role in the events leading to Jesus' crucifixion.

5. **Impact on Early Christians**: Following Jesus’ resurrection, early Christians, many of whom were Jewish, faced the challenge of defining their relationship with the established Jewish religious authorities. Initially, the early church adhered to Jewish laws, but as Gentiles were converted, theological discussions emerged about the relevance of the Law for salvation, as evidenced during the Jerusalem Council in Acts 15. The Pharisaic insistence on the Law influenced these vital debates.

In summary, the Pharisees’ strict adherence to the Laws of Moses greatly impacted their relationship with Jesus by fostering confrontation and theological disagreement.
