1. **Messianic Expectation**: In Jewish tradition, the Messiah was anticipated as a political and spiritual leader who would restore Israel, bring peace, and establish God's kingdom. Jesus fulfilled these expectations but in a way that transcended the immediate political aspirations of His time. 2. **Proclamation of the Kingdom**: Jesus' ministry is characterized by the proclamation of the kingdom. 3. **Inauguration of the Kingdom**: The New Testament presents Jesus as the inaugurated King of this kingdom. Through His sacrificial death and resurrection, Jesus achieved victory over sin and death, enabling a new relationship between humanity and God. His resurrection is seen as a definitive moment that marks the beginning of the fulfillment of God's kingdom, which is both a present reality and a future hope.

4. **Access to the Kingdom**: Jesus emphasizes that entrance into the kingdom is through faith in Him (John 3:3), and His role as the Messiah provides that access. 5. **Future Fulfillment**: While the kingdom is present in the teachings and actions of Jesus, Scripture also points to a future fulfillment when Christ will return to fully establish His reign, restore all creation, and eliminate sin and suffering (Revelation 21:1-4). This eschatological aspect underscores Jesus' role as the Messiah, who delivers spiritually in the here and now and promises a complete realization of God's kingdom in the future.

In summary, Jesus' role as the Messiah is intricately connected to the concept of the kingdom. He embodies the kingly authority of God, inaugurates the reign of God through His life and work, and invites all to be part of this kingdom both now and in the future.
