In Christian theology, the Bible describes Jesus as central to salvation in several key ways:

1. **Messiah and Savior**: Jesus is recognized as the Messiah (Christ) prophesied in the Old Testament. Isaiah 53 describes Him as the suffering servant who bears the sins of many. This fulfillment is affirmed in the New Testament, particularly in John 3:16, which states that God sent His Son to save humanity.

2. **Atonement**: The concept of atonement is foundational to salvation. Jesus' death on the cross is viewed as a sacrificial act that satisfies God's justice for sin. Romans 3:23-25 explains how all have sinned and fall short of God's glory, and that Jesus’ sacrifice serves as atonement through faith in His blood.

3. **Resurrection**: The resurrection of Jesus is crucial as it signifies His victory over sin and death. Romans 6:4 teaches that just as Christ was raised from the dead, believers too are given new life, presenting resurrection as pivotal for believers' hope of eternal life.

4. **Mediator**: Jesus serves as the mediator between God and humanity. According to 1 Timothy 2:5, "For there is one God and one mediator between God and mankind, the man Christ Jesus." This highlights His unique position in reconciling humanity to God.

5. **Faith in Christ**: Salvation is received through faith in Jesus. Ephesians 2:8-9 articulates that it is by grace through faith that we are saved, not by our own works. This underscores the belief that it is Jesus’ work that secures our salvation.

6. **Lordship**: Accepting Jesus as Lord is essential for salvation. Romans 10:9-10 emphasizes that confessing Him as Lord and believing in His resurrection leads to salvation, showcasing the relationship between faith and obedience.

In summary, the Bible depicts Jesus as the promised Savior whose sacrificial death and resurrection provide the means for humanity's reconciliation with God. This relationship is accessed through faith, establishing Him as the cornerstone of salvation for believers.
