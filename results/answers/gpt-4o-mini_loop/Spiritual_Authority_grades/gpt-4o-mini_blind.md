   - **Earthly Authorities**: These authorities are typically established through human institutions and societal structures, including government officials, law enforcement, and civic leaders. 2. **Purpose**:
   - **Spiritual Authorities**: Their primary purpose is to promote spiritual growth, guide believers in truth, and shepherd the faith community. Ephesians 4:11-13 describes the role of church leaders in equipping the saints for ministry and building up the body of Christ.

3. **Nature of Governance**:
   - **Spiritual Authorities**: Their governance is marked by servanthood, humility, and spiritual accountability. Jesus emphasized this in Mark 10:42-45, where He taught that true leadership involves serving others instead of exercising power over them.
   - **Earthly Authorities**: Although they can embody traits of servanthood, earthly authorities may be more focused on power structures, law enforcement, and institutional roles. This authority can sometimes lead to corruption or abuse, as depicted in various biblical narratives.

4. **Obedience and Accountability**:

   - **Earthly Authorities**: Christians are instructed to submit to earthly authorities (Romans 13:5) as long as such submission does not contradict God’s commands. They should also advocate for justice and righteousness within these structures, as encouraged in Micah 6:8.
