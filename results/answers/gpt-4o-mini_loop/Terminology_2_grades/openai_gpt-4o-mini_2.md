2. **Eucharist** – Derived from the Greek word "eucharistia," meaning "thanksgiving," this term is often used in liturgical traditions to highlight the act of giving thanks for Christ's sacrifice on the cross, reflecting the joy and gratitude of the Christian faith.

3. **The Breaking of Bread** – This phrase reflects the biblical accounts of Jesus breaking bread with His disciples, emphasizing fellowship, remembrance, and the shared experience of believers as a community in Christ.

5. **The Sacrament of the Altar** – Commonly used in Lutheran and some Reformed traditions, this term highlights the sacramental nature of the meal, recognizing it as a means of grace and a visible sign of God's promise.

6. **Mass** – Primarily used in Roman Catholic and some Anglican contexts, "Mass" refers to the celebration of the Eucharist, emphasizing the sacredness of the rite and the belief in the real presence of Christ in the elements.

7. **Communion Service** – This term designates the specific service or gathering where the Lord's Supper is celebrated, often used in Protestant traditions to promote collective worship and spiritual nourishment.

8. **The Divine Liturgy** – Used in Eastern Orthodox churches, this term refers to the formal service that includes the Eucharist, emphasizing the liturgical context and the theological significance of worshiping in spirit and truth.
