1. **Literal Meanings**:
   - In the Old Testament, the term is often associated with the Tabernacle, which served as the portable dwelling place of God among the Israelites, and later the Temple in Jerusalem, particularly Solomon's Temple. This aligns with the teaching that believers are the temple of the Holy Spirit (1 Corinthians 6:19) and signifies the communal aspect of worship and faith.

3. **Overall Significance**:

In summary, the "house of God" encompasses both the physical structures designated for worship and the collective body of believers who gather in His name, emphasizing the sacredness of these spaces and the continuous relationship God desires to have with His people throughout biblical history.
