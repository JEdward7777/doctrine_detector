In the context of biblical genealogies, such as those found in Genesis 5 and 11, "begat" is used to describe the relationship between an individual and their child, emphasizing that one generation gave rise to another.

In these accounts, "begat" typically indicates the following:

1. **Lineage**: It emphasizes the continuity of a family line or genealogical sequence, highlighting the connection between ancestors and descendants.
2. **Procreation**: The term often implies biological reproduction, underscoring the natural process of childbirth and the passing on of life from one generation to the next.
3. **Succession**: By linking one individual to another through "begat," the genealogies demonstrate how God's plan unfolded over time, with each person playing a role in the story of redemption.

When reading biblical genealogies, it's essential to understand that "begat" is not simply a dry recitation of names and dates. Rather, it serves as a framework for understanding God's relationship with His people throughout history, highlighting the covenants He made with them, and foreshadowing the coming of Jesus Christ, the ultimate descendant of Abraham (see Galatians 3:8-9).
