Being referred to as "children of God" has a profound impact on the relationship between believers and God. This concept is rooted in Scripture, particularly in John 1:12-13, where Jesus says, "But to all who did receive him, who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God—children born not of natural descent, nor of human decision or a husband's will, but born of God."

This designation as "children of God" signifies several key aspects of our relationship with God:

1. **Family Relationship**: Being referred to as children implies a familial relationship between believers and God. This connection is characterized by love, care, and intimacy, similar to the bond between earthly parents and their children.
2. **Inheritance**: As children of God, we inherit the blessings, promises, and privileges that come with being part of His family (Romans 8:17; Galatians 3:26). This inheritance is not limited to material possessions but includes spiritual riches, such as forgiveness, salvation, and eternal life.
3. **Spiritual Parenting**: As children of God, we are under the care and guidance of our Heavenly Father. He provides for our spiritual needs, disciplines us when necessary (Hebrews 12:5-11), and is always working to conform us into the image of His Son, Jesus Christ (Romans 8:29).
4. **Equality with Christ**: As children of God, we are considered co-heirs with Christ (Romans 8:17). This means that through our faith in Him, we have been given a share in His divine nature and inheritance.
5. **Dignity and Worth**: Being called "children of God" also emphasizes the inherent dignity and worth of each believer. We are no longer defined by our sinful past or earthly identity but are now seen as beloved children of the Most High God, worthy of love, respect, and protection.

In summary, being referred to as "children of God" signifies a deepening connection with Him, characterized by intimacy, inheritance, spiritual parenting, equality with Christ, and dignity. This designation serves as a powerful reminder of our position in Christ, our relationship with Him, and the incredible blessings that come with being part of His family.
