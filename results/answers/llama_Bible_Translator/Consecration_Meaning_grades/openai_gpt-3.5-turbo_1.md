For example, they would consecrate priests (Exodus 28:1-43) and the tabernacle (Exodus 25:1-31:18). This act of consecration signified that these things were now dedicated to God's service and purpose.

In the New Testament, Jesus Christ is the ultimate example of consecration. He was set apart by God the Father for a specific mission: to redeem humanity from sin (John 3:16). Through his life, death, and resurrection, Jesus consecrated himself to God's will, making him the perfect sacrifice for our sins.

This act of consecration involves prayer, laying on of hands, and a commitment to follow Jesus' teachings.
