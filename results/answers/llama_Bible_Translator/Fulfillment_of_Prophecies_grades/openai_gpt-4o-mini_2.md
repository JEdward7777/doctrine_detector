Prophecies are considered fulfilled when they match the actual events of history, demonstrating the accuracy and authority of God's revelation.

In the Bible, prophecies can be categorized into several types:

1. Direct prophecy: A clear prediction of a specific event or series of events that is later fulfilled.
2. Symbolic prophecy: A prophecy that uses figurative language to convey a message about future events. The meaning of these prophecies may not be immediately apparent, and their fulfillment might require interpretation.
3. Conditional prophecy: A prophecy that is conditional upon certain actions or circumstances taking place.

1. Specificity: Prophecies are often specific in their description of events, allowing for clear verification of fulfillment.
2. Clarity: The language used in the prophecy should be clear enough to understand what is being predicted.
3. Accuracy: The fulfillment of a prophecy must match the original prediction with a high degree of accuracy.
