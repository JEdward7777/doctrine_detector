In Matthew 25:41, Jesus describes hell as "eternal fire" prepared for the devil and his angels. In Luke 12:5, He warns that God will sentence those who reject Him to outer darkness, where there will be weeping and gnashing of teeth.

The concept of hell is rooted in Jewish apocalyptic literature, which envisioned a place of eternal punishment for those who rejected God's mercy. The Bible presents hell as a consequence of rejecting Jesus Christ and His salvation. Those who refuse God's love and do not repent will face eternal separation from Him (Matthew 25:46).

Some key passages to consider include:

1. Matthew 25:31-46 - Jesus teaches about the final judgment, where those who rejected Him will be sent away from His presence.
2. Mark 9:43-48 - Jesus warns that it's better to lose one's hand and enter eternal life with Christ than to retain both hands but suffer the fires of hell.
3. Luke 16:19-26 - The parable of Lazarus and the rich man illustrates the chasm between those who have received God's love and those who have not, resulting in eternal punishment.
4. Revelation 20:11-15 - John describes a lake of fire where those who have rejected God will be tormented day and night forever.

It's essential to note that the Bible teaches hell is not a place where people are punished for their sins, but rather a consequence of rejecting Jesus Christ and His salvation. The good news is that God desires all people to repent and come to Him (2 Peter 3:9), offering eternal life and forgiveness through faith in Jesus Christ.
