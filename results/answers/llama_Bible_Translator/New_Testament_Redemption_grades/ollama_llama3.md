In the Old Testament, purification was a central concept in Israelite religion and ritual practice. The primary purpose of purification was to remove ceremonial uncleanness or impurity that resulted from contact with something considered taboo or ritually unclean (e.g., death, menstruation, certain animals). This uncleanness had to be cleansed before one could re-enter the community and worship God.

There were several types of purification rituals in the Old Testament:

1. Levitical rites: The priests performed these rituals to cleanse individuals and objects from various types of impurity (e.g., Numbers 19:2-22; Leviticus 14:7-9).
2. Burn offerings: These sacrifices were offered to purify sinners and restore them to a state of righteousness (Leviticus 1:4; 16:20).
3. Water purification: The water of separation, also known as the "red heifer," was used to purify those who had come into contact with death or certain other unclean things (Numbers 19:2-10).

In contrast, in the New Testament, Jesus Christ's atoning sacrifice on the cross and his resurrection mark a significant shift in the concept of purification. Here are some key differences:

1. Christ as the ultimate purifier: In the New Testament, Jesus is portrayed as the one who cleanses believers from their sin and impurities (Acts 3:19; Hebrews 9:14).
2. Forgiveness of sins replaces ritual cleansing: With faith in Christ, believers are declared righteous and forgiven for their sins, making traditional purification rituals unnecessary (John 1:29; Romans 8:1-4).
3. Baptism supplants Levitical rites: In the New Testament era, baptism is seen as a symbol of one's union with Jesus Christ, replacing the need for ritual purifications like those in the Old Testament (Matthew 28:19; Acts 2:38).
4. Holiness by faith: The emphasis shifts from external rituals to internal holiness through faith in Jesus Christ (Romans 6:22-23; 1 Corinthians 3:17).

In summary, while purification was a crucial concept in the Old Testament, the New Testament presents Jesus Christ as the ultimate purifier who brings about forgiveness of sins and spiritual cleansing.
