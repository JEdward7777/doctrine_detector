A crucial question!

The Bible clearly teaches that repentance and the wrath of God are intimately connected. In fact, the two are flip sides of the same coin.

On one hand, the Bible declares that God is a just and righteous Judge who is angry with human sinfulness (Romans 1:18; Ephesians 2:3). This anger or "wrath" is not a capricious or arbitrary emotion, but rather a necessary expression of His holy character. As Psalm 7:11 says, "God is a just God, and there is no injustice in Him. He is righteous and does not wrong anyone."

On the other hand, repentance is turning away from sin and towards God (Acts 3:19; Luke 15:18-21). It involves acknowledging our sinfulness, confessing our guilt, and surrendering to Jesus Christ as Lord and Savior.

The connection between repentance and the wrath of God can be seen in several passages:

1. Romans 2:5 says, "But because of your stubbornness and your unrepentant heart, you are storing up wrath against yourself for the day of God's wrath." Here, Paul links the hardness of heart (or lack of repentance) to storing up wrath.
2. Luke 13:3-5 says, "Unless you repent, you will all perish. Like the bush that is burned but does not produce fruit, so too will it be with youâ€”unless you turn from your wicked ways and seek God." In this passage, Jesus warns of the consequences of refusing to repent.
3. Jonah 2:1-10 describes a repentant prophet who escapes God's wrath by turning to Him in prayer and fasting.

In all these passages, we see that repentance is not just a means of escaping God's wrath but also an essential step in appeasing His righteous indignation. When we turn from our sinfulness and towards God, we acknowledge the severity of our offenses against His holiness and seek mercy through Jesus Christ (Habakkuk 2:4; Luke 15:18-21).

In summary, the Bible teaches that repentance is not a way to avoid God's wrath but rather an acknowledgment of our sinfulness and a turning towards Him in trust. As we repent, we are actually appeasing His righteous indignation and experiencing mercy through Jesus Christ.
