The New Testament authors stress the importance of these witnesses in establishing the truth about Jesus' life, death, and resurrection. For example:

1. The Gospels themselves are often referred to as "eyewitness accounts" or "testimonies" (Luke 1:2-3).

3. The book of Hebrews emphasizes the importance of eyewitness testimony, noting that the apostles were chosen to be witnesses to Jesus' life and resurrection (Hebrews 2:16).

In Christian theology, the witness testimony of these individuals serves as a foundation for our understanding of Jesus' true nature, His teachings, and His sacrifice on the cross. It also provides evidence for the reality of His resurrection, which is central to the Christian faith.
