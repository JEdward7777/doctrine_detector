Jesus used several parables to describe the value and nature of the Kingdom of God. Here are some examples:

1. The Parable of the Sower (Matthew 13:1-23, Mark 4:1-20, Luke 8:5-15) - This parable describes the different responses people have to the message of the Kingdom, with some receiving it and bearing fruit, while others fail to produce fruit due to lack of understanding or spiritual hardening.
2. The Parable of the Mustard Seed (Matthew 13:31-32, Mark 4:30-32) - This parable illustrates how the Kingdom begins small but grows into something mighty, becoming a source of blessing and nourishment for all who come to it.
3. The Parable of the Yeast (Luke 13:18-21) - Jesus used this parable to describe how the Kingdom of God is like yeast that spreads through dough, leavening it and making it rise. This emphasizes the transformative power of the Kingdom in people's lives.
4. The Parable of the Treasure Hidden in a Field (Matthew 13:44-46) - This parable highlights the worthlessness of earthly treasures compared to the priceless value of the Kingdom of God, which is found through repentance and faith.
5. The Parable of the Pearl of Great Value (Matthew 13:45-46) - Similar to the previous one, this parable emphasizes the immense value of the Kingdom of God, which is worth more than all the treasures in the world.

These parables, along with others like the Parable of the Net (Matthew 13:47-50), the Parable of the Wheat and the Tares (Matthew 13:24-30), and the Parable of the Good Samaritan (Luke 10:25-37), reveal the essence and character of the Kingdom of God, which is all about God's redemptive plan to save humanity through faith in Jesus Christ.
