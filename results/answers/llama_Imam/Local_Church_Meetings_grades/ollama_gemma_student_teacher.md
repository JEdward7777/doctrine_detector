In Islamic tradition, believers typically meet for congregational prayers and other religious activities in the following ways:

1. Friday Prayer (Jumu'ah): On Fridays, Muslims gather at the mosque for a special congregational prayer led by an Imam. This is one of the most important communal prayers in Islam.
2. Daily Prayers: Muslims pray five times a day, individually or in groups, depending on their location and availability. Many people attend the mosque for these daily prayers, especially during peak prayer hours (e.g., Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, and Isha).

4. Eid Prayers: On special occasions like Eid al-Fitr (celebrating the end of Ramadan) and Eid al-Adha (celebrating the pilgrimage), Muslims gather at the mosque for congregational prayers, often led by an Imam.

These gatherings provide opportunities for socializing, learning, and spiritual growth within the Muslim community.
