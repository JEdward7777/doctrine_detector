Firstly, the apostles recognized the importance of establishing a strong foundation for the ummah (community) based on faith, unity, and mutual support. They spent considerable time building relationships with one another, sharing their knowledge and experiences, and relying on each other's strengths.

One notable example is the relationship between the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) and his closest companion, Abu Bakr (may Allah be pleased with him). Their bond was built on trust, loyalty, and a deep understanding of each other's characters. This type of strong foundation allowed them to weather any challenge or trial that came their way.

Secondly, the apostles were diligent in sharing knowledge and wisdom with one another. They engaged in discussions, debates, and even disagreements, all while maintaining a spirit of humility and a willingness to learn from one another.

We see this in the story of the Companions, where they would gather around the Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) to listen to his teachings and seek guidance. This emphasis on knowledge-sharing and mutual learning helped to create a culture of intellectual curiosity and spiritual growth within the community.

Thirdly, the apostles demonstrated extraordinary compassion, empathy, and concern for one another's well-being. They recognized that every individual has unique strengths and weaknesses, and they worked together to support those who were struggling or in need.

The example of Umar ibn Al-Khattab (may Allah be pleased with him) is particularly noteworthy. He was known for his generosity and kindness towards the poor and marginalized. His actions inspired others to follow suit, creating a ripple effect of charity and kindness within the community.

Lastly, the apostles understood the importance of forgiveness, mercy, and reconciliation. When conflicts arose, they would seek to resolve them through open communication, apologies, and amends. This approach helped to maintain harmony within the community and prevented the spread of division or strife.

In conclusion, the apostles' actions in teaching and fostering community among believers offer valuable lessons for us today. By building strong relationships, sharing knowledge and wisdom, showing compassion and empathy, and practicing forgiveness and reconciliation, we can create a community that is rooted in faith, unity, and mutual support.

May Allah guide us on this path, and may we be inspired by the examples of our beloved apostles (peace be upon them).
