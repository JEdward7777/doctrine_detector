In Christian tradition, particularly in Protestant and Catholic interpretations, a "Christian" is often defined as someone who:

1. Professes faith in Jesus Christ as the Son of God and Savior.
2. Recognizes him as the Messiah and the central figure of salvation history.
3. Has been baptized in the name of the Father, the Son, and the Holy Spirit (Matthew 28:19-20).
4. Embodies the fruits of the Spirit, such as love, joy, peace, patience, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, gentleness, self-control, and humility (Galatians 5:22-23).

The biblical account presents Jesus Christ as the embodiment of God's love and salvation for humanity. To be a "Christian" in this sense means to have responded to God's invitation to receive salvation through faith in Jesus and to demonstrate that faith through obedience to his teachings.
