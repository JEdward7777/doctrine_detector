The Bible defines compassion as a deep love and desire to help others, especially those who are suffering or in need. It is a commandment to love one another as Jesus loves us (John 3:16). Compassion is also mentioned in the Bible as a fruit of the Holy Spirit (Galatians 5:22).

Some actions that are associated with being compassionate include:

1. Praying for and blessing others
2. Serving others in love and humility
3. Helping those in need, including the poor, sick, and widows
4. Forgiving others and seeking forgiveness
5. Speaking kind words and encouraging others
6. Offering comfort and support to those who are hurting
7. Loving and caring for others as Jesus loves us
8. Sharing the Gospel message with others
9. Protecting and defending those who are persecuted or oppressed
10. Finding ways to serve and bless others in the name of Jesus.
