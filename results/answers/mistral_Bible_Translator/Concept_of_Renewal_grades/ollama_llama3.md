Restoration or renewal in this context refers to the hope that humanity, and even creation itself, can be restored to its original pristine state through redemption and sanctification.

2. Prophetic Restoration: The Old Testament prophets often spoke of restoration as a renewal or revival for God's people Israel. New Covenant Restoration (Jeremiah 31:31-34): The New Testament teaches that the coming of Jesus Christ and His sacrifice established a new covenant between God and humanity, offering renewal and restoration to all who believe in Him. 5. Restoration of All Things (Acts 3:20-21): In the Book of Acts, Peter speaks of a future time when God will restore all things, bringing about peace and harmony. This renewal will encompass not only humanity but all creation, ultimately fulfilling the original purpose for which it was created (Revelation 21-22).

So, in summary, 'restoration' as 'renewal' in a Christian context refers to God's work of restoring His creation to its original purpose and perfection. This includes individual renewal through salvation, restoration of relationships, and the ultimate restoration of all things at Christ's return.
