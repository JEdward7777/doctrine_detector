 In Christian theology, consecration (or dedication) is the setting apart of an individual or object for divine use or service. The act of consecration signifies a deep commitment to God and His purposes. It involves surrendering one's self or something to God in obedience, submission, and worship.

This is typically found in both Old Testament (e.g., Numbers 6:2-8) and New Testament teachings (e.g., 1 Corinthians 7:17). Consecration prepares the individual or thing for God's service by establishing a bond between them and God, making them holy, and enabling them to serve their intended purpose according to God's will.

This is not only limited to people but also extends to physical objects like tabernacles, temples, churches, and items used in worship (e.g., the Ark of the Covenant, Aaron's priestly garments). By consecrating these objects, they become sanctified, set apart for God, and are made fit to be used in His service.

Through consecration, individuals and things come under God's protection, guidance, and blessing, ensuring that they fulfill their purpose according to the divine plan.
