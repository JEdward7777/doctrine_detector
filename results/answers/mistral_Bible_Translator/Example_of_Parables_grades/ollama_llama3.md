Here's a simplified version of the Parable:

A sower went out to sow seeds in his field. Some seeds fell on the path, some on rocky ground, others among thorns, and still others on good soil. The seeds that fell on the path were quickly snatched away by birds. The seeds sown on rocky ground sprang up quickly but withered under the sun because they had no roots. The seeds sown among thorns grew up along with the thorns, which eventually choked them. However, the seeds sown on good soil yielded a harvest of thirty, sixty or even a hundred times what was sown.
