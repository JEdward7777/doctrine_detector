In contrast, the New Testament introduces a shift in focus from external purification to internal purification, emphasizing spiritual cleansing through faith in Jesus Christ (Acts 22:16; Hebrews 9:13-14). Therefore, purification becomes a matter of the heart rather than just adhering to external rules (Matthew 15:8-9; Mark 7:6).

The Holy Spirit is then sent to indwell each believer, enabling them to live a life pleasing to God and empowering them to overcome sin (Romans 8:1-17; Galatians 5:16-25).

This shift in the concept of purification from the Old Testament to the New Testament is an important part of understanding the transition from the Law of Moses to grace through faith in Jesus Christ.
