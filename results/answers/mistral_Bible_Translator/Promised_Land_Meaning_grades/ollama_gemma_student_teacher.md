 In the context of God's covenant with Abraham, as recorded in the Hebrew Bible (Old Testament), the "Promised Land" refers to the land of Canaan. This was a region encompassing parts of modern-day Israel, Palestine, Jordan, and parts of Egypt and Syria.

God made an unconditional promise to Abraham (Genesis 12:7; 15:18) that his descendants would inherit this land, even though at that time it was inhabited by other nations. This promise was reiterated throughout the Old Testament as a significant aspect of God's covenant with Abraham and his descendants, particularly through Isaac (Genesis 26:3-5) and Jacob (Genesis 28:10-22).

The Promised Land signifies a physical territory that was a key part of God's plan for Abraham's descendants, the Israelites. However, it also symbolizes a spiritual dimension, representing God's provision, protection, and faithfulness to his people throughout their history.
