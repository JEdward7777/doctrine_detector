 In Christian teachings, being "saved" or "salvation" refers to the act of deliverance from sin and its consequences, including eternal separation from God. This salvation is made possible through Jesus Christ, who died on the cross as a sacrifice for the sins of humanity and rose again to offer eternal life (John 3:16).

The Bible teaches that all have sinned and fall short of God's glory (Romans 3:23), and because sin separates us from God, we are unable to earn our way into a right relationship with Him through good works or moral living. Salvation is a free gift from God, received by grace through faith in Jesus Christ (Ephesians 2:8-9).

In the spiritual sense, being saved means being made new creations in Christ, receiving forgiveness of sins, and being granted eternal life. It also means being transformed by the Holy Spirit to live a life that is pleasing to God and bearing fruit for His kingdom (Galatians 5:22-23).

Salvation is not just a one-time event but an ongoing process of sanctification, in which believers are made more like Christ as they grow in their relationship with Him. Ultimately, being saved is about experiencing the fullness of God's love, grace, and mercy, and spending eternity in His presence.
