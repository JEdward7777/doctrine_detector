Temptation refers to the act of enticing or alluring someone towards sinful or disobedient behavior, often initiated by an external agent such as Satan or one's own desires (James 1:13-14). Temptation presents a choice between following God's commands and yielding to sinful desires. Jesus was tempted in the wilderness but did not give in (Matthew 4:1-11).

Testing, on the other hand, has a different connotation. It is often used to describe situations where God allows difficulties or trials to strengthen one's faith and character (James 1:2-4). The intent behind testing is not to lead someone into sin but rather to produce endurance, maturity, and spiritual growth in the believer.
