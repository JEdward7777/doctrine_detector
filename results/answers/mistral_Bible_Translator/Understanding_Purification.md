1. To protect the Israelites from physical and spiritual harm caused by impurity.
2. To remind the people of their need for atonement and forgiveness, as well as their dependence on God's grace.
3. To create a sense of separation between the holy (clean) and profane (unclean) within the Israelite community.
4. To instill in the people an awareness of the holiness of God and the need for obedience to His commands.
