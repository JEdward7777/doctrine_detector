 In Christian theology, as reflected in the Bible, the term "Word of God" encompasses several but interconnected meanings:

1. Logos (Λόγος) in Greek New Testament: This is a central concept in John's Gospel, where it refers to Jesus Christ, who is the divine expression and revelation of God. He is the embodiment of God's truth and wisdom. In this context, Jesus is often referred to as "the Word" (John 1:1, 14).

2. The Scriptures: In a broader sense, the Bible itself is also considered the Word of God. It contains God's revelation to humanity and provides guidance for Christian living and understanding of God. This includes both the Old Testament (Hebrew Bible) and the New Testament (Christian Bible).

3. Prophetic utterances: Specific spoken or written messages from God through prophets, like those recorded in the Old Testament books of Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel, etc., are also considered part of the Word of God.

4. Oral Tradition: Some Christian denominations acknowledge certain oral traditions or Church teachings as the "Word of God," such as oral instructions passed down through apostolic succession or deuterocanonical books in some versions of the Bible.

In summary, the term 'Word of God' encompasses Jesus Christ, the Scriptures, prophetic utterances, and certain oral traditions or teachings, each playing a unique role in revealing and embodying the nature and will of God to humanity.
