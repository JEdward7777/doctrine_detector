Another example can be found in the story of Cain and Abel (Genesis 4:1-16). When Cain murdered his brother out of jealousy, God confronted him about his sin. Cain's offering to God was rejected, and as a result, he was condemned to wander the earth, away from the presence of God.

In the New Testament, Jesus Christ's teachings emphasize repentance and forgiveness for sins. In John 8:11, Jesus tells the woman caught in adultery, "Neither do I condemn you; go, and from now on sin no more." The story illustrates that while the woman was a sinner, she could seek forgiveness and strive to avoid repeating her mistakes.

In summary, the Bible illustrates the consequences of guilt and sin as physical hardship, emotional turmoil (e.g., fear, shame), social isolation, and even spiritual death (separation from God). It also presents a message of hope, emphasizing repentance, forgiveness, and the possibility of making amends for past mistakes to live a better life moving forward.
