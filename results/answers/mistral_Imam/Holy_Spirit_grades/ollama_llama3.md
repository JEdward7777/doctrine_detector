In Islamic belief, the Holy Spirit is not a part of a triune deity; rather, it is an angelic being.

When introducing the concept of the Holy Spirit in contexts unfamiliar with the Trinity, I would explain that the Quran uses the term Ruh al-Qudus to refer to an angelic messenger sent by God for a specific purpose - in this case, to announce to Maryam (Mary) the birth of Isa (Jesus). The Holy Spirit is believed to be a creation of God, not equal or coeternal with Him.

I would also highlight that in Islam, the belief in angels and their roles as messengers between God and His creatures is an essential aspect of our faith. Angels are mentioned frequently throughout the Quran, emphasizing their role in carrying out God's will on earth.

In this way, we can explain the concept of the Holy Spirit in a Muslim context to those unfamiliar with the Trinity, focusing on its unique Islamic understanding and emphasizing its role as an angelic messenger from God.
