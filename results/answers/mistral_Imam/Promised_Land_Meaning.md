 In the Islamic context, the term "Promised Land" or "Al-Ard Al-Mubarakah" (The Blessed Land) signifies the land that God promised to give to the Children of Israel and later, to Prophet Ibrahim (Abraham) and his offspring. This concept is mentioned in several verses of the Quran.

According to Islamic belief, the promise was fulfilled when Prophet Musa (Moses) led the Israelites out of Egypt and into Canaan, which is now modern-day Palestine. However, it's important to note that the Islamic understanding does not restrict this promise exclusively to the Children of Israel, but rather sees it as a broader prophecy regarding the land from which Prophet Ibrahim (peace be upon him) and his family originated.

In Surah Al-Baqarah (Chapter 2, Verse 124), Allah says: "And when his Lord tried Abraham with [certain] words, he fulfilled them. He said, 'Indeed, you will not settle until you reach the end of the world.' Indeed, his Lord was seeing him."

This verse indicates that God made a promise to Ibrahim about settling in a specific land (which could be interpreted as "the end of the world"), and Ibrahim fulfilled this promise. However, the exact interpretation can vary based on different scholarly opinions.
