Forgiveness, on the other hand, is God's graceful response to repentant hearts. In the Bible, particularly in the Old Testament, there are numerous instances where God calls upon His people to repent and turn away from their sins, promising forgiveness and restoration (e.g., Jonah 3:10).

In the New Testament, Jesus Christ's ministry often involved calling people to repentance, emphasizing God's mercy, love, and readiness to forgive those who turned back to Him (Matthew 9:13). This concept is beautifully encapsulated in Acts 3:19, where Peter says, "Repent therefore, and turn again, that your sins may be blotted out."

It's essential to note that the Bible also emphasizes the importance of forgiving others as we seek God's forgiveness. However, unlike Christianity, there is no concept of Jesus Christ mediating between the sinner and Allah in Islam.
