 In Christianity, as presented in the New Testament of the Bible, the term "Lord" (Kyrios in Greek) is often used to refer to God. However, it is also used to address Jesus Christ, signifying his divine nature, Messiahship, and authority. This use of the title reflects the belief in the Trinity - one God existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.

Regarding Jesus, Christians believe that he is the Son of God who came to earth as a human being, lived among people, died on the cross for humanity's sins, and was resurrected. They see him as an essential part of the divine nature of God, not separate from it but one with the Father.
