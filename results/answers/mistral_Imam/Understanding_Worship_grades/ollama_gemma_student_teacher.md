 In Islam, the concept of worship (Ibada) encompasses a wide range of actions that bring us closer to Allah (God). It is essentially submitting ourselves willingly to the will of Allah in every aspect of our lives. This submission is reflected in five fundamental practices (Arkani), known as the Five Pillars of Islam, which are the Declaration of Faith (Shahada), Prayer (Salah), Zakat (charity), Fasting during Ramadan (Sawm), and Pilgrimage to Makkah (Hajj) once in a lifetime for those who are able.

However, worship goes beyond these five practices. It includes all actions that are done sincerely for the pleasure of Allah, such as reading the Quran, performing good deeds, showing kindness, and avoiding what is forbidden (Haram). Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) said, "Deeds are considered by the intention, and a person will get the reward according to his intention." (Sahih Muslim 2564)

In essence, worship in Islam means establishing a strong and continuous bond with Allah through obedience, love, respect, and submission. It is about striving for His pleasure, seeking His guidance, and trying our best to live according to His teachings in all aspects of life.
