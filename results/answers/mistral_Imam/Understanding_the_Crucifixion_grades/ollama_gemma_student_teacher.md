The central theme of Christianity revolves around the belief that Jesus Christ, who Christians believe to be the Son of God, came to earth to save humanity from sin through his death and resurrection.

The crucifixion is seen as Jesus' voluntary sacrifice for humanity, where he took upon himself the sins of mankind to provide redemption. This belief is based on the New Testament, specifically the Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John), which describe Jesus' trial, condemnation, and crucifixion by the Romans.

For Christians, the crucifixion symbolizes Jesus' love for humanity, his obedience to God's will, and his role as a savior. The events surrounding Jesus' crucifixion, including his last supper with his disciples and his resurrection three days later, are all significant in Christian theology.
