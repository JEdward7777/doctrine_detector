 In Christian teachings, both "foreordain" and "predestine" are terms often used to describe God's sovereignty and His role in determining the course of human events. However, there is a subtle difference between the two.

"Predestine" generally refers to God deciding ahead of time what will happen, typically in regard to salvation or election (the choice of some individuals for eternal life). This idea is often associated with Calvinism and the doctrine of predestination.

On the other hand, "foreordain" implies a planning or determining of events that are already known to God. It suggests a prearrangement or predetermination, but not necessarily in the sense of absolute decree as found in some interpretations of predestination. In this context, it emphasizes the sovereignty and wisdom of God rather than focusing on the specifics of what is decreed.
