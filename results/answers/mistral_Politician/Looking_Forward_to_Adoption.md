Romans 8:23 (KJV) states, "And not only they, but ourselves also, which have the firstfruits of the Spirit, even we ourselves groan within ourselves, waiting for the adoption, to wit, the redemption of our body."

In this passage, Paul is speaking to the Roman believers about the struggle between the Spirit and sin in their lives. The "adoption" or "sonship" refers to a legal status bestowed on those who believe, making them children of God (Galatians 4:5). This status is not yet fully realized in this life, as believers are still living with bodies that are subject to sin and death.

The "redemption of our body" refers to the hope for glorification or transformation of their earthly bodies, which will occur when Jesus returns (1 Corinthians 15:42-49). This new body will be free from the effects of sin and death, and believers will experience perfect communion with God.

For modern believers, this passage serves as a reminder of their ongoing struggle against sin and a confirmation of their hope for redemption in Christ's return. It encourages them to persevere through hardships and wait patiently for the fulfillment of their ultimate adoption as children of God.
