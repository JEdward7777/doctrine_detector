 In Christian context, 'ministry' refers to the service or care provided by a member of the church or clergy to others, particularly in spiritual matters. The term is derived from the Greek word "diakonia," which means serving or ministering. This service can take various forms, such as preaching, teaching, counseling, healing, and acts of compassion.

The goal of ministry, according to the Bible, is to bring people closer to God by sharing His love, forgiveness, and salvation. It's about meeting physical, emotional, and spiritual needs, guiding others in their faith journey, and encouraging spiritual growth. Jesus exemplified this through His compassionate care for people, healing the sick, comforting the bereaved, and teaching with wisdom.

In a broader sense, ministry can also refer to any service or act of kindness shown to others, as all believers are called to be ministers in their own way. This is reflected in James 1:27, "Religion that God our Father accepts as pure and faultless is this: to look after orphans and widows in their distress and to keep oneself from being polluted by the world."

In all forms of ministry, the aim is to show love, compassion, and care towards others, while striving to honor God in our actions.
