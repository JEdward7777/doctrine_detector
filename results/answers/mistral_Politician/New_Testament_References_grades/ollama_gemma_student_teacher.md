 In the New Testament, there is no explicit statement that identifies John the Baptist as a Nazirite. The Nazirite vow, as described in Numbers 6:2-8, involves abstaining from wine and grape products, not cutting your hair during the period of the vow, and avoiding any contact with dead bodies.

However, there are hints that suggest John the Baptist may have followed some aspects of the Nazirite lifestyle. In Luke 1:15, it is said of John before his birth that he will be "filled with the Holy Spirit" even before his birth, which could be seen as a precursor to taking the Nazirite vow. Additionally, in Matthew 3:4 and Mark 1:6, John the Baptist is described as eating locusts and wild honey, a diet allowed for Nazirites under Numbers 28:40. Lastly, in all four canonical Gospels, there is no mention of John the Baptist ever having his hair cut, although this could be due to the conventions of biblical narrative rather than historical accuracy.

It's important to note that this interpretation is not universally accepted and scholars have varying opinions on the subject. Furthermore, the New Testament does not provide definitive evidence one way or another regarding John the Baptist being a Nazirite according to Jewish law.
