 In religious contexts, 'manna' symbolizes the miraculous daily provision of food for the Israelites during their journey through the desert in the wilderness of Sinai, as detailed in the Book of Exodus and continued throughout the books of Numbers and Deuteronomy in the Hebrew Bible. The word 'manna' is derived from the Hebrew term "mann," meaning "what is it?"

The manna was described as a small, round, white, sweet bread-like substance that appeared on the ground each morning, except on Sabbaths, when double portions were provided for the previous two days. This miraculous provision sustained the Israelites during their 40 years of wandering in the desert and served as a tangible reminder of God's continuous care, protection, and providence over His chosen people.

In modern discussions, it serves as an allegory for divine guidance and sustenance throughout life's journeys, highlighting the belief that God provides for all our needs when we place our trust in Him.
