 In the context of Paul's New Testament writings, particularly those found in the books of Romans, 1 Corinthians, and Philippians, confession that Jesus is Lord is closely tied to salvation. This concept is rooted in the Greco-Roman cultural milieu where "Lord" (Kyrios) was a title given to emperors or powerful figures, reflecting submission and allegiance. When Paul uses the phrase "Jesus is Lord," he is asking his readers to acknowledge Jesus' authority and deity, and to submit themselves to him as their spiritual ruler.

In Romans 10:9, Paul writes, "If you declare with your mouth, 'Jesus is Lord,' and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved." This passage emphasizes both the verbal confession (declaring Jesus as Lord) and the belief (faith) in Jesus' resurrection as essential components for salvation.

Paul's letters suggest that he views salvation as a free gift available through faith in Jesus Christ. However, it is important to note that this understanding requires contextualization within its historical and cultural framework. The relationship between confessing that Jesus is Lord and being saved according to Paul's writings represents one of the central themes in his teachings and has been the subject of extensive scholarly debate.
