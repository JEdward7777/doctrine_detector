 In a Christian context, the act of consecration refers to setting aside something or someone for sacred or religious purposes, often under the guidance and protection of God or a particular saint. The process of consecration may involve prayers, rituals, and formal ceremonies, which are meant to sanctify the object or individual being consecrated.

For example, in Catholicism, a church building can be consecrated by a bishop as part of the process of establishing a new parish. Similarly, religious items such as rosary beads, crucifixes, and holy water fonts may also be consecrated for their sacred use.

In addition to physical objects, individuals can also be consecrated in various ways, such as when someone is ordained as a priest or bishop, or when an individual takes religious vows as a monk, nun, or member of a religious order. In this sense, the act of consecration signifies a person's dedication to living a life in service to God and the Church.
