The four canonical New Testament Gospels — Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John — are traditionally attributed to these four evangelists. This usage of the term refers to the specific role as the authors of the sacred texts, rather than a more general sense of spreading the Christian faith as a missionary or preacher.
