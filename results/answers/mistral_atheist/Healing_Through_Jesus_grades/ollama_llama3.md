2. Compassion: Jesus expressed compassion towards the individuals, understanding their suffering. In Matthew 14:14, when he saw the crowds suffering, he felt compassion for them, healing their sick. This is also shown in Mark 5:19, where after healing a man possessed by legions of demons, Jesus told him to return home and "tell how much the Lord has done for you" so that others may be healed as well.

3. Exorcism: The method used by Jesus for exorcising demons involved a command or prayer. In Mark 1:25, we read that Jesus said to a man with an unclean spirit, "Be quiet! Come out of him!" and the demon obeyed. In other instances, like in Luke 4:33-36, the demons would cry out during the exorcism, revealing their identity.

4. Restoration: Once the demons were expelled, Jesus often focused on restoring the individual to wholeness. This could involve social reintegration, as in Mark 5:19 (mentioned above), and teaching them about God's love and commandments, such as instructing a healed leper to follow the law (Mark 1:40-45).

It is essential to note that these accounts are presented within the religious and cultural context of first-century Palestine. Jesus' actions should be understood in the light of Jewish exorcism traditions, apocalyptic beliefs, and the Hellenistic worldview of demonic possession.
