 In Christian teachings, "the Last Day" or "Doomsday" is a term that refers to the end of the world as described in various religious texts, primarily in the Bible. This concept can be found in several passages across different books of the New Testament, including Matthew 24:36, Mark 13:32-37, Luke 12:35-40, Acts 1:11, and Revelation 20:11-15. However, interpretations can vary among different Christian denominations.

The Last Day is often associated with the Second Coming of Christ and includes a series of events such as the rapture (the catching away of faithful believers), the seven-year tribulation period (a time of great suffering), and the millennium (a thousand years of peace).
