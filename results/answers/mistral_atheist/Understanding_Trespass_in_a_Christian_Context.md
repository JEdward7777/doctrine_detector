 In a Christian worldview, the concept of trespass and sin are closely related as they both revolve around the idea of transgressing God's moral laws or disobeying His commandments. The Bible uses the term "trespass" to denote sins committed against one another (Matthew 5:23-24, Matthew 6:12, James 4:7). This is seen in the parable of the Good Samaritan where a man was robbed and left for dead on the side of the road, depicting how humans have trespassed against one another by failing to help those in need (Luke 10:25-37).
