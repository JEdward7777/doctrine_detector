 In Christian Biblical tradition, the phrase "Word of God" is often used in two primary ways:

1. As a direct reference to Jesus Christ. In John 1:1, it is stated, "In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God." In this context, the Word (Logos in Greek) is synonymous with Jesus, who is considered divine.

2. As a metaphorical reference to Scripture itself. In 2 Timothy 3:16-17, it is written, "All Scripture is breathed out by God and profitable for teaching, for reproof, for correction, and for training in righteousness." Here, the term "Scripture" (graphe in Greek) refers to the holy books of the Bible.

However, interpretations may vary across different Christian denominations and faith traditions, so it's essential to consider context when discussing these concepts.
